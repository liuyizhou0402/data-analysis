# U4.L04 · Changes in Signaling Pathways 信号通路的改变（学生版）

> **本课你将学会 / By the end you can:**
> ① 预测突变、药物如何影响信号通路
> ② 区分激动剂（agonist）与拮抗剂（antagonist）
> ③ 把通路扰动连接到疾病与药物作用机制

---

## 📌 一句话本质 / In One Sentence
> **改变通路上任何一环（突变、药物、毒素），都会沿着箭头改变下游响应——这正是很多药物和疾病的作用原理。**

---

## 一、为什么通路会改变 / Sources of Change

| 来源 | 机制 |
|------|------|
| **突变（mutation）** | 受体/激酶等蛋白结构改变 → 功能增强、减弱或丧失 |
| **药物（drugs）** | 人为结合受体或通路分子，模拟或阻断信号 |
| **毒素（toxins）** | 如霍乱毒素锁定 G 蛋白于激活态 |

---

## 二、激动剂 vs 拮抗剂 / Agonist vs Antagonist ⭐

| | **激动剂 Agonist** | **拮抗剂 Antagonist** |
|---|---|---|
| 作用 | **模拟**配体、激活受体 | **阻断**受体、阻止配体结合 |
| 效果 | 增强信号响应 | 减弱信号响应 |
| 例子 | 某些止痛药模拟内啡肽 | β-受体阻断剂（降血压）|

> 💡 **记忆**：agonist = "帮手"（激活）；antagonist = "对手"（阻断）。

---

## 三、突变对通路的影响 / Mutation Effects ⭐

> 高频 FRQ：给一个通路某环节突变，预测下游后果。

| 突变类型 | 后果 |
|----------|------|
| 受体**持续激活**（不需配体也开） | 下游响应**持续开启**（如某些癌症，接 L08）|
| 受体**失活**（无法结合配体） | 下游响应**无法开启** |
| 激酶**持续激活** | 通路常开 |
| 抑制性分子失活 | 通路失去"刹车"，持续激活 |

**推理方法**：
1. 定位突变在通路的哪一步
2. 判断该步是被"卡在开"还是"卡在关"
3. 顺着箭头推下游的最终响应

---

## 四、临床/真实联系 / Real-World Links

- **霍乱**：霍乱毒素使 G 蛋白持续激活 → cAMP 过量 → 肠道大量排水 → 腹泻脱水
- **癌症**：生长信号通路突变持续激活 → 细胞不停分裂（接 L08）
- **药物**：很多药物是受体的激动剂或拮抗剂（如抗组胺药是组胺受体拮抗剂）

## 七、本课练习 / Practice

### Part A · 基础 MCQ

**A1.** A molecule that binds a receptor and mimics the natural ligand, activating the pathway, is called
(A) an antagonist  (B) an agonist  (C) a phosphatase  (D) a second messenger

**A2.** A receptor antagonist
(A) activates the receptor  (B) blocks the receptor, preventing the normal ligand from acting
(C) increases signal amplification  (D) adds phosphate groups

**A3.** A mutation causes a growth-factor receptor to be permanently active even without its ligand. The MOST likely result is
(A) no cell growth  (B) continuous activation of the growth pathway
(C) the receptor cannot bind ligand  (D) the pathway shuts down

**A4.** Cholera toxin locks a G protein in its active state, leading to excessive cAMP. This is an example of
(A) a mutation in DNA  (B) a toxin altering a signaling pathway
(C) normal feedback regulation  (D) receptor antagonism

**A5.** A drug that blocks a receptor would be expected to
(A) increase the cell's response to the natural ligand  (B) decrease the cell's response to the natural ligand
(C) have no effect  (D) become a second messenger

### Part B · 进阶 MCQ

**B1.** In a pathway: Growth factor → Receptor → Relay proteins → Cell division. A tumor cell has a mutation making the relay protein permanently active. **Predict** the consequence.
(A) The cell will stop dividing  (B) The cell will divide continuously, even without growth factor
(C) The receptor will not function  (D) Growth factor production will increase

**B2.** Beta-blockers are drugs that bind to epinephrine receptors on heart cells without activating them, preventing epinephrine from binding. Beta-blockers act as
(A) agonists  (B) antagonists  (C) second messengers  (D) kinases

### Part C · 真题风格 FRQ

**C1.** *[题型：Analyze Model + Conceptual Analysis｜4 分｜建议 10 分钟]*

A signaling pathway controls cell division: **Growth factor → Receptor → Kinase cascade → Transcription of cell-division genes**.

(a) **Predict** the effect on cell division if a mutation causes the receptor to be permanently (constitutively) active. **Explain** your reasoning. *(2)*

(b) **Predict** the effect on cell division if a drug acts as an antagonist that blocks the receptor. *(1)*
