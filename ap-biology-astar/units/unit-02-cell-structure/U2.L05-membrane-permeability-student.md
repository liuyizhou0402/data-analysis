# U2.L05 · Membrane Permeability 膜的选择透过性（学生版）

> **本课你将学会 / By the end you can:**
> ① 解释什么决定一个分子能否穿过膜（大小、极性、电荷）
> ② 区分"自由扩散"和"需要蛋白质协助"的物质
> ③ 为 L06–L08 各种运输方式打下判断基础

---

## 📌 一句话本质 / In One Sentence
> **膜的疏水核心是"安检口"——小而非极性的放行（自由扩散），大、带电、极性的拦下（需要蛋白质通道）。**

---

## 一、决定通透性的三要素 / What Determines Permeability

一个分子能否自由穿过磷脂双层的**疏水核心**，取决于：

| 要素 | 易穿过 | 难穿过 |
|------|--------|--------|
| **大小（size）** | 小分子 | 大分子 |
| **极性（polarity）** | 非极性 | 极性 |
| **电荷（charge）** | 不带电 | 带电（离子） |

> 🔑 **逻辑**：疏水核心排斥极性/带电物质（它们更"喜欢"水）。非极性小分子能溶进脂质层穿过去。

---

## 二、各类物质的通透性分级 / Permeability Ranking

| 物质类型 | 例子 | 能否自由扩散 |
|----------|------|--------------|
| 小非极性 | O₂、CO₂、N₂ | ✅ 自由扩散（快） |
| 小不带电极性 | H₂O、尿素、甘油 | ⚠️ 缓慢扩散（水可慢扩散，也走水通道 aquaporin） |
| 大不带电极性 | 葡萄糖、蔗糖 | ❌ 需要载体蛋白 |
| 离子 | Na⁺、K⁺、Cl⁻、H⁺ | ❌ 需要通道蛋白（且带电，最难） |

> ⚠️ **特别注意水**：水是极性的，理论上不易穿过疏水核心，但因为分子**很小**，可以缓慢通过；细胞还有专门的**水通道蛋白（aquaporins）**加速水的运输（接 L07 渗透）。

---

## 三、为什么这很重要 / Why It Matters

膜的选择透过性是细胞**维持内环境稳定（homeostasis）**的基础：
- 把需要的留住、把废物排出
- 在膜两侧建立**浓度梯度**（如 Na⁺/K⁺ 梯度）→ 用于神经传导、营养吸收等（接 L08 主动运输、U4 信号）

---

## 四、知识串联 / Connections

- ⬆ 上游：L04 磷脂双层的疏水核心；U1.L02 极性/非极性、官能团
- ⬇ 下游：
  - 能自由扩散的 → L06 简单扩散
  - 需要蛋白的 → L06 易化扩散（顺梯度）/ L08 主动运输（逆梯度）
  - 水 → L07 渗透与水势
- 🌐 大概念：**结构（疏水核心）决定功能（选择透过）**

---

## 五、考点提示 / Exam Tips

- 🎯 **判断题高频**：给一个分子（带电？多大？极性？），判断它怎么过膜。
- 🧠 **判断口诀**：小+非极性 = 自己过；大/极性/带电 = 找蛋白。
- ⚠️ 水是例外——极性但小，能慢扩散 + 有水通道。

## 六、本课练习 / Practice

### Part A · 基础 MCQ

**A1.** Which property makes a molecule LEAST able to cross the phospholipid bilayer without help?
(A) Small size  (B) Nonpolar nature  (C) Carrying an electric charge  (D) Being a gas

**A2.** Which of the following can diffuse most readily across a cell membrane without a transport protein?
(A) Na⁺  (B) Glucose  (C) CO₂  (D) A protein

**A3.** Water is a polar molecule, yet it can slowly cross the membrane because it
(A) is nonpolar  (B) is very small and can also pass through aquaporin channels
(C) is actively pumped  (D) dissolves the membrane

**A4.** Charged ions such as K⁺ require channel proteins to cross the membrane because
(A) they are too small to be detected  (B) their charge is repelled by the hydrophobic core of the bilayer
(C) they are nonpolar  (D) they are gases

**A5.** Selective permeability of the membrane is important because it allows the cell to
(A) grow indefinitely  (B) maintain homeostasis by controlling what enters and exits
(C) eliminate the need for organelles  (D) stop all molecular movement

### Part B · 进阶 MCQ

**B1.** A new synthetic molecule is large, polar, and uncharged. Based on membrane permeability principles, how will it MOST likely cross the membrane?
(A) Freely by simple diffusion through the bilayer  (B) It will require a transport protein
(C) It cannot interact with cells at all  (D) By dissolving the phospholipids

**B2.** A toxin blocks ALL channel and carrier proteins in a cell's membrane but leaves the phospholipid bilayer intact. Which substance could STILL cross the membrane?
(A) Glucose  (B) Na⁺ ions  (C) O₂  (D) Amino acids

**B3.** Cells lining the kidney tubules need to reabsorb large amounts of water rapidly. These cells have many aquaporins. **Explain** why aquaporins are necessary even though water can slowly diffuse across the membrane on its own.

### Part C · 真题风格 FRQ

**C1.** *[题型：Conceptual Analysis｜3 分｜建议 8 分钟]*

A researcher tests the ability of four substances to cross an artificial phospholipid bilayer with no proteins: oxygen gas (O₂), glucose, potassium ions (K⁺), and a steroid hormone (nonpolar).

(a) **Predict** which substances will cross the bilayer freely and which will not. *(1)*

(b) **Explain** your predictions based on the size, polarity, and charge of each substance and the structure of the bilayer. *(2)*
