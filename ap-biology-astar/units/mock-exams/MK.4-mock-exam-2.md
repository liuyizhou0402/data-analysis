## Section II · Free Response（6 题）

**Q1.** *[Interpreting & Evaluating Experimental Results]*

Potato cores are placed in sucrose solutions of different molarities; percent change in mass is recorded after 24 h:

| Sucrose (M) | % mass change |
|:---:|:---:|
| 0.0 | +16 |
| 0.2 | +7 |
| 0.4 | −2 |
| 0.6 | −13 |
| 0.8 | −22 |

(a) **Explain** why the cores gained mass at 0.0 M and lost mass at 0.8 M. *(2)*
(b) **Determine** the approximate molarity at which the cells are isotonic to the solution. **Justify** using the data. *(2)*
(c) **Calculate** the solute potential (Ψs) of a 0.4 M sucrose solution at 25°C (i = 1, R = 0.0831). Show work. *(2)*
(d) **Evaluate** one way to improve the reliability of this experiment. *(2)*

**Q2.** *[Interpreting Results with Graphing]*

Predator (wolves) and prey (moose) populations are tracked over 5 years (in hundreds): Year 1: moose 60, wolves 10; Year 2: moose 80, wolves 14; Year 3: moose 50, wolves 20; Year 4: moose 30, wolves 15; Year 5: moose 45, wolves 9.

(a) **Construct** a graph showing both populations over time. *(4)*
(b) **Describe** the relationship between predator and prey populations. *(2)*
(c) **Explain** how this represents density-dependent regulation. *(2)*

**Q3.** *[Scientific Investigation]* **Design** a controlled experiment to test how pH affects the activity of the enzyme catalase. Identify the hypothesis, IV, DV, control group, and one controlled variable. *(4)*

**Q4.** *[Conceptual Analysis]* A population of insects is sprayed with a pesticide. Most die, but a few survive and the population becomes resistant. **Explain**, using the three conditions of natural selection, how this resistance evolves. *(4)*

**Q5.** *[Analyze Model]* An mRNA reads 5'-AUG-GCA-AAG-UAA-3'. (a) **Determine** the number of amino acids in the resulting polypeptide. (b) **Explain** the role of the UAA codon. (c) A mutation deletes the first A of the second codon — **identify** the mutation type and **predict** its effect. *(4)*

**Q6.** *[Analyze Data]* Community X: species A=25, B=25, C=25, D=25. Community Y: species A=85, B=5, C=5, D=5 (both total 100). (a) **Calculate** Simpson's index (D = 1 − Σ(n/N)²) for each. (b) **Determine** which community is more diverse. (c) **Explain** how diversity relates to ecosystem stability. *(4)*
