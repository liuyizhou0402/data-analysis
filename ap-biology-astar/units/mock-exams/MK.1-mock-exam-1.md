## Section I · Multiple Choice（24 题｜建议 36 分钟）

### Unit 1 · Chemistry of Life

(A) basic  (B) acidic  (C) nonpolar  (D) hydrophobic throughout

**3.** Starch and cellulose are both polymers of glucose but differ in function because
(A) they contain different monomers  (B) cellulose contains nitrogen
(C) of the type of glycosidic linkage (α vs β)  (D) starch is a protein

### Unit 2 · Cell Structure & Function

**4.** A cell specialized for secreting digestive enzymes would have abundant

**5.** A cell placed in a hypertonic solution will

**6.** The sodium-potassium pump moving ions against their gradients is an example of
(A) simple diffusion  (B) active transport  (C) facilitated diffusion  (D) osmosis

### Unit 3 · Cellular Energetics

**7.** The oxygen released during photosynthesis comes from the splitting of
(A) carbon dioxide  (B) glucose  (C) water  (D) ATP

(A) glycolysis  (B) the Krebs cycle  (C) fermentation  (D) oxidative phosphorylation

**9.** A competitive inhibitor's effect can be reduced by
(A) adding more substrate  (B) lowering temperature  (C) lowering pH  (D) removing the enzyme

### Unit 4 · Cell Communication & Cell Cycle

**10.** A steroid hormone is most likely to bind to
(A) a membrane receptor  (B) an intracellular receptor  (C) a carbohydrate  (D) DNA polymerase

**11.** Insulin lowering blood glucose after a meal is an example of
(A) positive feedback  (B) an antagonist  (C) negative feedback  (D) signal amplification

**12.** A mutation that makes a tumor-suppressor gene (e.g., p53) nonfunctional could lead to cancer because
(A) cell division is permanently stopped  (B) the cell makes too much p53

### Unit 5 · Heredity

(A) anaphase I of meiosis  (B) mitosis anaphase  (C) anaphase II of meiosis  (D) metaphase

## Section II · Free Response（6 题）

> Q1–Q2 长题（各约 8–10 分，~25 分钟）；Q3–Q6 短题（各 4 分，~10 分钟）。成段作答。

**Q1.** *[Interpreting & Evaluating Experimental Results]*

A scientist measures the rate of cellular respiration (O₂ consumed, mL/min) in germinating seeds at different temperatures:

| Temp (°C) | O₂ consumed (mL/min) |
|:---:|:---:|
| 10 | 0.5 |
| 20 | 1.2 |
| 30 | 2.4 |
| 40 | 3.1 |
| 50 | 1.0 |

(a) **Describe** the relationship between temperature and respiration rate. *(2)*
(b) **Explain**, at the molecular level, why the rate drops sharply between 40°C and 50°C. *(2)*
(c) **Justify** why measuring O₂ consumption is a valid measure of aerobic respiration. *(2)*
(d) **Design** a controlled experiment to test whether germinating seeds respire faster than dormant seeds at 25°C (identify IV, DV, one control variable). *(2)*

**Q2.** *[Interpreting Results with Graphing]*

A population of bacteria grows in unlimited nutrients. Counts: 0 h = 100, 1 h = 200, 2 h = 400, 3 h = 800, 4 h = 1600.

(a) **Construct** a graph of population size vs. time. *(4)*
(b) **Identify** the type of growth and **explain** how the data support your answer. *(2)*
(c) **Predict** how the curve would change if a carrying capacity of 2000 were imposed. *(2)*

**Q3.** *[Scientific Investigation]* **Design** an experiment to test how light intensity affects the rate of photosynthesis in aquatic plants. Identify the hypothesis, IV, DV, control, and how you would measure the rate. *(4)*

**Q4.** *[Conceptual Analysis]* **Explain** how the lac operon allows *E. coli* to express lactose-digesting enzymes only when lactose is present. Include the roles of the repressor and operator. *(4)*

**Q5.** *[Analyze Model]* A signaling pathway: **Hormone → Receptor → Kinase A → Kinase B → Response**. (a) **Explain** how this pathway amplifies the signal. (b) **Predict** the effect on the response if Kinase A is inhibited. (c) **Predict** the effect if a mutation makes the receptor permanently active. *(4)*

**Q6.** *[Analyze Data]* In a Hardy-Weinberg population of 500 beetles, 45 are homozygous recessive (bb) for body color. (a) **Calculate** q and p. (b) **Calculate** the expected number of heterozygotes. (c) **Predict** how predation on bb beetles would change q over time. *(4)*
