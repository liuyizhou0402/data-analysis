## 📌 一句话本质 / In One Sentence
> **所有生命共享一个祖先（证据藏在保守的分子里），而进化从未停止——抗生素抗性、农药抗性就在我们眼前实时发生。**

---

## 一、共同祖先的证据 / Evidence for Common Ancestry ⭐

所有生物共享的特征 → 指向**单一共同祖先**：

| 共享特征 | 说明 |
|----------|------|
| **遗传物质都是 DNA** | 储存、复制方式相同 |
| **通用遗传密码** | 同样的密码子→氨基酸（接 U6.L05）|
| **核糖体翻译** | 都用核糖体合成蛋白 |
| **ATP 作为能量货币** | 共同的能量系统（接 U3）|
| **保守的核心基因** | 如 rRNA 基因在所有生物中相似 |

> 🔑 越**保守（conserved）**的序列，越古老、越重要——它们在差异极大的物种间仍相似，因为来自共同祖先且突变会致命。

---

## 二、进化在持续发生 / Continuing Evolution ⭐

> 进化不是"过去的事"——它**现在仍在发生**，且可观测：

### 抗生素抗性 Antibiotic Resistance（经典例）
1. 细菌群体中存在抗性变异（来自突变，随机）
2. 使用抗生素 = 选择压力 → 杀死敏感菌，抗性菌存活
3. 抗性菌繁殖 → 抗性等位基因频率上升
4. 几代后整个群体抗性 → **进化发生了**

> ⚠️ 滥用抗生素加速抗性进化——这是公共卫生大问题。

### 其他实时进化例子
- **杀虫剂抗性**（害虫）
- **除草剂抗性**（杂草）
- **HIV 对药物的抗性**（病毒高突变率 → 快速进化）
- **桦尺蛾（工业黑化）**：工业革命污染→深色蛾更隐蔽→频率上升

---

## 三、为什么抗性进化这么快 / Why So Fast

- 微生物**世代时间短**（细菌几十分钟一代）
- **群体数量巨大** → 变异多
- **强选择压力**（抗生素/农药）
- 这些条件使等位基因频率快速改变

## 六、本课练习 / Practice

### Part A · 基础 MCQ

**A1.** The near-universal genetic code shared by almost all organisms is evidence for
(A) convergent evolution  (B) a common ancestor of all life  (C) artificial selection  (D) genetic drift

**A2.** Antibiotic resistance in bacteria evolves because
(A) antibiotics cause bacteria to mutate on purpose  (B) resistant variants already present survive and reproduce while susceptible ones die
(C) all bacteria become resistant equally  (D) bacteria choose to resist

**A3.** Highly conserved DNA sequences (similar across very different species) suggest that those sequences
(A) are unimportant  (B) arose recently  (C) are ancient and inherited from a common ancestor  (D) evolve rapidly

**A4.** Why does antibiotic resistance evolve quickly in bacteria?
(A) Bacteria have long generation times  (B) Short generation times, huge populations, and strong selection pressure
(C) Bacteria do not reproduce  (D) There is no variation

**A5.** The peppered moth becoming darker during the industrial revolution is an example of
(A) evolution that can be observed over short timescales  (B) evolution that stopped long ago
(C) artificial selection  (D) convergent evolution

**B2.** A patient stops taking antibiotics early, before all bacteria are killed. The surviving bacteria are most likely
(A) all susceptible  (B) those with greater resistance, which then reproduce  (C) genetically identical to the dead ones  (D) unable to reproduce

**B3.** HIV evolves drug resistance rapidly within a single patient largely because the virus
(A) has a very low mutation rate  (B) has a high mutation rate, generating variation that selection acts on
(C) does not reproduce  (D) cannot change
