## 📌 一句话本质 / In One Sentence
> **群落里的物种通过竞争、捕食、共生互相影响——这些关系塑造了谁能在哪生存（生态位）以及群落的结构。**

---

## 一、种间关系总览 / Species Interactions ⭐

> 用对双方的利(+)/害(−)/无影响(0)来分类：

| 关系 | 物种A | 物种B | 说明 |
|------|:---:|:---:|------|
| **竞争 Competition** | − | − | 争夺有限资源 |
| **捕食 Predation** | + | − | 捕食者得利、猎物受害 |
| **互利共生 Mutualism** | + | + | 双方都获益 |
| **偏利共生 Commensalism** | + | 0 | 一方获益、另一方无影响 |
| **寄生 Parasitism** | + | − | 寄生者得利、宿主受害 |

---

## 二、三种共生 / Three Symbioses ⭐

| 类型 | 关系 | 例子 |
|------|------|------|
| **互利共生 Mutualism** | +/+ | 蜜蜂与花（传粉↔花蜜）；根瘤菌与豆科植物（固氮↔养分）；肠道菌 |
| **偏利共生 Commensalism** | +/0 | 藤壶附着鲸鱼（藤壶得运输，鲸无影响）|
| **寄生 Parasitism** | +/− | 绦虫与宿主；蚊子吸血 |

> 💡 共生（symbiosis）= 两物种长期密切共同生活，包括以上三类。

---

## 三、生态位与竞争排斥 / Niche & Competitive Exclusion ⭐

- **生态位（niche）**：一个物种在生态系统中的"角色"——它如何利用资源（食物、空间、时间等）
- **竞争排斥原理（competitive exclusion）**：两个生态位**完全相同**的物种不能长期共存——竞争力强的会排除另一个
- **资源分隔（resource partitioning）**：物种通过分化生态位（利用不同资源/时间）减少竞争、共存
  - 例：不同鸟在树的不同高度觅食

| 概念 | 含义 |
|------|------|
| 基础生态位 | 理论上能占据的全部范围 |
| 实际生态位 | 因竞争实际占据的范围 |

---

## 四、捕食与协同进化 / Predation & Coevolution

- 捕食驱动**协同进化（coevolution）**：捕食者与猎物相互施加选择压力
  - 猎物进化防御（拟态、毒素、警戒色、保护色）
  - 捕食者进化更强捕猎能力
- **防御适应**：拟态（mimicry）、伪装（camouflage）、化学防御

## 七、本课练习 / Practice

### Part A · 基础 MCQ

**A1.** A relationship in which both species benefit is
(A) parasitism  (B) commensalism  (C) mutualism  (D) competition

**A2.** Barnacles attaching to a whale, gaining transport while the whale is unaffected, is an example of
(A) mutualism  (B) commensalism  (C) parasitism  (D) predation

**A3.** The competitive exclusion principle states that
(A) two species with identical niches cannot coexist indefinitely  (B) all species can coexist
(C) competition never occurs  (D) predators always win

**A4.** A tapeworm living in a host's intestine, absorbing nutrients and harming the host, is an example of
(A) mutualism  (B) commensalism  (C) parasitism  (D) competition

**A5.** A species' niche is
(A) only its physical location  (B) its role and how it uses resources in the ecosystem
(C) the same for all species  (D) its population size

### Part B · 进阶 MCQ

**B1.** Two species of birds feed at different heights in the same trees, reducing competition. This is an example of
(A) competitive exclusion  (B) resource partitioning  (C) mutualism  (D) parasitism

**B2.** Nitrogen-fixing bacteria live in the roots of legume plants; the bacteria receive sugars and the plant receives usable nitrogen. This is
(A) commensalism  (B) parasitism  (C) mutualism  (D) predation

**B3.** A prey species evolves a toxic chemical defense, and over time predators evolve resistance to it. This reciprocal evolutionary change is called
(A) competitive exclusion  (B) coevolution  (C) commensalism  (D) resource partitioning

### Part C · 真题风格 FRQ

**C1.** *[题型：Conceptual Analysis｜4 分｜建议 10 分钟]*

Species in a community interact in many ways.

(a) **Distinguish** among mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism in terms of how each species is affected. *(2)*
(b) **Explain** the competitive exclusion principle and **describe** how resource partitioning can allow similar species to coexist. *(2)*
