# U8.L08 · Disruptions to Ecosystems 生态系统扰动（学生版）

> **本课你将学会 / By the end you can:**
> ① 解释入侵物种如何破坏生态系统
> ② 描述人类活动对生态系统的影响
> ③ 解释气候变化的生态后果

---

## 📌 一句话本质 / In One Sentence
> **生态系统能承受一定扰动，但入侵种、人类活动和气候变化可能超过其恢复力，引发连锁的、有时不可逆的崩溃。**

---

## 一、入侵物种 / Invasive Species ⭐

- **入侵物种（invasive species）**：被引入非原生地、快速扩散并危害当地生态的物种
- **为什么危害大**：
  - 缺乏天敌/竞争者 → 不受控增长（接 L03 指数增长）
  - 与本地种竞争资源（竞争排斥，接 L05）
  - 捕食本地种、传播疾病
- 例：亚洲鲤鱼、葛藤、棕树蛇（关岛）、水葫芦

> 🔁 串联 L03：入侵种初期常呈指数增长，因为新环境无密度依赖限制。

---

## 二、人类活动的影响 / Human Impacts ⭐

| 活动 | 后果 |
|------|------|
| **栖息地破坏** | 砍伐、城市化 → 物种失去家园、多样性下降 |
| **污染** | 化学物质、塑料、富营养化 |
| **过度开发** | 过度捕捞、狩猎 → 种群崩溃 |
| **引入入侵种** | 见上 |

**富营养化（eutrophication）**：
- 农业径流（氮、磷）→ 水体营养过剩 → 藻类暴发（藻华）
- 藻类死亡被分解 → 耗尽溶解氧 → 鱼类死亡（缺氧区 dead zones）

---

## 三、气候变化 / Climate Change ⭐

- **温室气体**（CO₂、CH₄）增加 → 全球变暖
- 来源：化石燃料燃烧（接 U3 呼吸/碳循环）、森林砍伐
- **生态后果**：
  - 物种分布改变（向极地/高海拔迁移）
  - 物候错位（如开花与传粉者不同步）
  - 海洋酸化（CO₂ 溶入海水）→ 危害珊瑚、贝类
  - 极端天气、栖息地丧失

---

## 四、生态系统恢复力 / Resilience

- **恢复力（resilience）**：生态系统受扰动后恢复的能力
- 高多样性 → 高恢复力（接 L07）
- 超过临界点（tipping point）→ 可能不可逆地转变为另一种状态

---

## 五、知识串联 / Connections

- ⬆ 上游：L03 指数增长（入侵种）；L05 竞争排斥；L07 多样性与稳定性；U3 碳循环/呼吸
- ⬇ 下游：U7.L11 灭绝（人类驱动的第六次大灭绝）；保护生物学
- 🌐 大概念：**人类对生态系统的影响**

## 六、考点提示 / Exam Tips

- 🎯 **必考**：入侵种为什么能爆发（无天敌、指数增长、竞争排斥）。
- 🎯 富营养化机制（营养→藻华→缺氧→鱼死）。
- 🎯 气候变化的生态后果（分布改变、海洋酸化）。
- 🎯 多样性↑→恢复力↑。

## 七、本课练习 / Practice

### Part A · 基础 MCQ

**A1.** Invasive species often cause severe damage to ecosystems because they
(A) have many natural predators  (B) lack natural predators/competitors and can grow unchecked
(C) cannot reproduce  (D) only eat each other

**A2.** Eutrophication of a lake from fertilizer runoff leads to
(A) increased oxygen  (B) algal blooms followed by oxygen depletion and fish death
(C) more fish  (D) clearer water

**A3.** A major cause of recent global climate change is
(A) decreased CO₂  (B) increased greenhouse gases from burning fossil fuels
(C) volcanic eruptions only  (D) reduced sunlight

**A4.** Ocean acidification, caused by CO₂ dissolving in seawater, particularly harms
(A) sharks  (B) coral and shellfish (which build calcium carbonate structures)  (C) seaweed  (D) bacteria

**A5.** An ecosystem's ability to recover after a disturbance is called its
(A) carrying capacity  (B) resilience  (C) niche  (D) trophic level

### Part B · 进阶 MCQ

**B1.** When an invasive species is introduced to a new area, it often initially grows
(A) logistically, immediately limited by K  (B) exponentially, because density-dependent limits (predators, competitors) are absent
(C) not at all  (D) only as fast as native species

**B2.** Why does higher biodiversity tend to increase an ecosystem's resilience to disturbances?
(A) It reduces the number of species  (B) Functional redundancy means other species can compensate if some are lost
(C) It eliminates all competition  (D) It prevents any change

**B3.** Warming temperatures cause a plant to flower earlier, but its pollinator insect emerges at its usual time, so they no longer overlap. This "mismatch" is a consequence of
(A) eutrophication  (B) climate change disrupting timing (phenology)  (C) invasive species  (D) succession

### Part C · 真题风格 FRQ

**C1.** *[题型：Conceptual Analysis + Argumentation｜4 分｜建议 10 分钟]*

Human activities can disrupt ecosystems in various ways.

(a) **Explain** why an invasive species can spread rapidly and harm a native ecosystem, referring to population growth and species interactions. *(2)*
(b) **Explain** how fertilizer runoff can lead to fish die-offs through eutrophication. *(2)*
