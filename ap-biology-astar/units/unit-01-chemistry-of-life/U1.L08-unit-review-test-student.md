## 一、3C 串联回顾：结构决定功能的 5 个经典案例

> 这 5 个案例是 Unit 1 → 整本书的桥梁，熟记后可应对任何题型变体。

| 分子 | 结构特征 | 功能 | 接后续单元 |
|------|---------|------|-----------|
| **水** | 极性 + 氢键 | 内聚/高比热/溶剂 | U2 渗透；U4 体温稳态 |
| **磷脂** | 两亲性（亲水头 + 疏水尾） | 自发形成双分子层 | **U2 细胞膜骨架** |
| **蛋白质** | 氨基酸序列→三维折叠 | 酶/受体/运输/结构 | **U3 酶；U4 受体；U2 膜蛋白** |
| **纤维素 vs 淀粉** | β vs α 糖苷键 | 结构（不可消化）vs 储能（可消化） | 消化/代谢 |
| **DNA** | 互补双链+反向平行+碱基配对 | 精确复制遗传信息 | **U6 复制/转录/翻译** |

## 二、易错点总温习 / Common Mistakes Final Review

| 知识点 | 错误版本 | 正确版本 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 氢键位置 | "H 键在 H₂O 分子内 O–H 之间" | H 键在**分子之间** |
| 低温 vs 高温对酶（对蛋白质） | "低温也让蛋白变性" | 低温减慢但**不变性**；高温才变性（不可逆） |
| 脂质是聚合物 | "脂质 = 甘油三酯是单体聚合" | 脂质**不是**标准聚合物；不满足"相同单体"定义 |
| 纤维素单体是果糖 | "纤维素 = 果糖" | 纤维素 = **β-葡萄糖**（与淀粉同是葡萄糖，但键型不同）|
| DNA 互补链方向 | "直接写碱基，忘记标方向" | 必须标 5'→3' 方向 |
| 蛋白质变性 = 断肽键 | "高温断裂肽键" | 高温破坏**氢键/疏水/离子键**；**肽键通常不断** |

## 三、单元测评 / Unit 1 Assessment

> **说明**：本测评为完整单元检测。MCQ 部分在纸上完成；FRQ 用成段英文作答。
> 建议时间：50 分钟（MCQ 20 分钟 + FRQ 30 分钟）

### Section I · MCQ（10 题，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which property of water is MOST directly responsible for the transport of water from the roots of a tall tree to its leaves?
(A) High specific heat  (B) Cohesion and adhesion  (C) Evaporative cooling  (D) Low density of ice

**2.** Glucose, fructose, and galactose share the molecular formula C₆H₁₂O₆ but have different structures. Which term BEST describes their relationship?
(A) Polymers  (B) Isomers  (C) Enantiomers  (D) Isotopes

**3.** A protein loses its function when treated with a detergent that disrupts hydrophobic interactions. Which level of protein structure is MOST directly affected?
(A) Primary  (B) Secondary  (C) Tertiary  (D) Quaternary

**4.** Adding a hydroxyl group (–OH) to an otherwise nonpolar molecule would MOST likely
(A) make the molecule less soluble in water  (B) increase the molecule's ability to form hydrogen bonds with water
(C) eliminate the molecule's ability to interact with other molecules  (D) convert the molecule into a lipid

**5.** Which of the following correctly describes a key difference between DNA and RNA?
(A) DNA contains uracil; RNA contains thymine  (B) DNA uses ribose; RNA uses deoxyribose
(C) DNA is typically double-stranded; RNA is typically single-stranded  (D) DNA stores protein; RNA stores genetic information

**6.** A cell is placed in a hypotonic solution. Based on the osmotic properties of water, what would you expect to happen?
(A) Water moves out of the cell; the cell shrinks  (B) Water moves into the cell; the cell may lyse
(C) No net movement of water; the cell stays the same  (D) Solute moves into the cell to equalize concentrations

**7.** Glycogen and starch are both made of α-glucose. The fact that glycogen is more highly branched than starch is an adaptation that allows glycogen to
(A) store more total glucose  (B) be digested more slowly
(C) release glucose more rapidly when energy is needed  (D) dissolve more easily in plant cell sap

**8.** A mutation changes one amino acid in an enzyme's active site from a polar to a nonpolar residue. What is the MOST likely effect on the enzyme?
(A) No effect, as primary structure changes do not affect function  (B) The enzyme's activity would increase because nonpolar residues stabilize the structure
(C) Substrate binding might be disrupted, reducing enzymatic activity  (D) The enzyme's primary structure would be destroyed

**9.** The phospholipid bilayer of a cell membrane is described as a "fluid mosaic." Which structural feature of phospholipids MOST directly contributes to the fluidity of the bilayer?
(A) The hydrophilic heads, which are positively charged  (B) Unsaturated fatty acid tails, which prevent tight packing due to double-bond kinks
(C) The glycerol backbone, which forms cross-links between phospholipids  (D) The phosphate group, which binds adjacent phospholipids together

**10.** A DNA molecule has 200 base pairs, of which 80 pairs are G–C. What is the total number of hydrogen bonds holding the two strands together?
(A) 200  (B) 280  (C) 360  (D) 440

### Section II · FRQ（2 题，共 8 分）

**FRQ 1.** *[Short FRQ — Conceptual Analysis｜4 分｜建议 12 分钟]*

The graph below is described as follows: it shows the rate of a chemical reaction over time, with and without an enzyme, both starting with the same amount of substrate. The enzyme-catalyzed reaction reaches equilibrium faster and shows a much steeper initial rate than the uncatalyzed reaction.

(a) **Identify** what property of enzymes explains the difference in reaction rate between the two curves. *(1)*

(b) **Explain** at the molecular level how an enzyme speeds up a reaction without being consumed. *(2)*

(c) **Predict** what would happen to the enzyme-catalyzed curve if the experiment is repeated at a temperature 30°C above the enzyme's optimum. **Justify** using protein structure concepts from this unit. *(1)*

---

**FRQ 2.** *[Short FRQ — Conceptual Analysis｜4 分｜建议 12 分钟]*

A biology student makes the following claim: "Since starch and cellulose are both composed entirely of glucose, they should have the same properties and functions."

(a) **Identify ONE** structural difference between starch and cellulose at the molecular level that refutes this claim. *(1)*

(b) **Explain** how this structural difference results in different digestibility in humans. *(2)*

(c) **Use** the concept of "structure determines function" to explain why cellulose is well-suited for its role in the plant cell wall, referring to at least one specific structural feature. *(1)*
