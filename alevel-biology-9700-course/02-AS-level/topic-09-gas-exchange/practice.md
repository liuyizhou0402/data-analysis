## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which feature is found in ALL efficient gas exchange surfaces?
- A. Countercurrent flow of fluids
- B. A thin, moist surface with a large surface area
- C. A circulatory system to transport gases
- D. Muscle-driven ventilation

**2.** What role does surfactant play in the alveoli?
- A. It increases the thickness of the alveolar wall to provide mechanical support
- B. It reduces surface tension, preventing alveoli from collapsing
- C. It actively transports oxygen from the alveolus into the blood
- D. It catalyses the conversion of CO₂ to carbonic acid

**3.** During inspiration, why does air flow into the lungs?
- A. Muscular contraction directly pushes air into the trachea
- B. The diaphragm moves upward, compressing the lungs
- C. Lung volume increases and pressure inside the lungs falls below atmospheric pressure
- D. The rib cage moves inward, decreasing lung volume

**4.** Which muscles contract during normal (quiet) inspiration?
- A. Internal intercostal muscles and diaphragm
- B. External intercostal muscles and diaphragm
- C. Abdominal muscles and diaphragm
- D. External intercostal muscles and abdominal muscles

**5.** In fish, blood and water flow in opposite directions over the gills. What is the advantage of this arrangement?
- A. It allows the fish to extract oxygen faster by increasing the speed of water flow
- B. It maintains a concentration gradient for O₂ diffusion along the entire gill surface
- C. It prevents CO₂ from entering the water
- D. It ensures that haemoglobin becomes fully saturated with O₂ before leaving the gill

**6.** In insects, which statement about gas exchange is correct?
- A. O₂ is carried by the blood (haemolymph) to all tissues
- B. O₂ diffuses through spiracles, along tracheae, to cells
- C. Spiracles are always open to ensure a continuous supply of O₂
- D. Tracheoles are sealed at their ends to prevent water loss

**7.** A student uses a spirometer with a CO₂ absorber. After 5 minutes of breathing, the trace shows a baseline that has gradually dropped by 2 litres. What does this represent?
- A. The volume of CO₂ exhaled
- B. The volume of O₂ consumed by the subject
- C. The tidal volume of the subject
- D. The residual volume of the lungs

**8.** Which feature of alveoli directly reduces the diffusion distance for O₂?
- A. The presence of surfactant in the alveolar fluid
- B. The single layer of squamous epithelial cells forming the alveolar wall
- C. The large number of alveoli per lung
- D. The high rate of blood flow through pulmonary capillaries

**9.** In which organism would you expect to find gas exchange occurring directly at cells, without a blood transport system for O₂?
- A. A fish
- B. A rabbit
- C. A grasshopper
- D. A frog

**10.** A student calculates that cell A has a surface area of 7.5 μm² and volume of 1.5 μm³. Cell B has surface area of 18 μm² and volume of 9 μm³. Which cell relies more on diffusion for gas exchange, and why?
- A. Cell B; it has a larger surface area
- B. Cell A; it has a higher SA:V ratio
- C. Cell A; it has a smaller volume
- D. Cell B; it has a higher SA:V ratio

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** A scientist measures the oxygen content of water entering a fish's mouth and leaving the gills, and simultaneously measures the oxygen content of blood entering and leaving the gill capillaries.

| Location | O₂ content / cm³ per 100 cm³ |
|---|---|
| Water entering mouth | 0.9 |
| Water leaving gills | 0.15 |
| Blood entering gills | 0.2 |
| Blood leaving gills | 0.8 |

   (a) Calculate the percentage of oxygen extracted from the water by the fish. Show your working. **[2]**

   (b) Explain how the data in the table supports the presence of a countercurrent exchange system. **[3]**

   (c) Predict what the O₂ content of blood leaving the gills would be if the fish used parallel flow instead of countercurrent flow. Explain your reasoning. **[2]**

**2.** A student uses a spirometer to record breathing at rest, then during exercise.

| Condition | Tidal volume / dm³ | Breathing rate / min⁻¹ |
|---|---|---|
| Rest | 0.5 | 12 |
| Exercise | 1.8 | 24 |

   (a) Calculate the ventilation rate (total volume per minute) at rest and during exercise. **[2]**

   (b) Describe and explain why both tidal volume and breathing rate increase during exercise. **[3]**

   (c) The spirometer contained a CO₂ absorber. Suggest why the trace showed a downward drift in baseline over time, and calculate approximately how much O₂ was consumed in 5 minutes at rest. **[3]**
