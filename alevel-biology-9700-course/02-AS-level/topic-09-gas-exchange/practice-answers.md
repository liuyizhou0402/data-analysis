| 3 | **C** | 膈肌收缩→容积↑→压力↓→空气流入。A 肌肉不直接推空气；B/D 均描述错误方向。 |
| 4 | **B** | 静息吸气：外肋间肌 + 膈肌收缩。A 内肋间肌是用力**呼气**；C/D 均含腹肌（呼气用）。 |
| 5 | **B** | 逆流保证全程浓度梯度→持续扩散。A 速度不是关键；C CO₂ 也有扩散；D 不一定 100% 饱和，但效率高。 |
| 6 | **B** | 昆虫气管系统：O₂ 经气门→气管→气管小管直接扩散到细胞。A 昆虫血淋巴不运 O₂；C 气门不总是开（防水分散失）；D 气管小管末端湿润（开放）。 |
| 7 | **B** | 加 CO₂ 吸收剂后，呼出 CO₂ 被吸收，基线下降量 = **O₂ 消耗量**。 |
| 8 | **B** | 鳞状上皮（squamous epithelium）极薄 → 扩散距离最短。A surfactant 减表面张力；C/D 影响速率但不是扩散距离。 |
| 9 | **C** | 昆虫（grasshopper）有气管系统，O₂ 直接扩散到细胞。A/B/D 均用血液/血液循环运输 O₂。 |
| 10 | **B** | Cell A: SA:V = 7.5/1.5 = 5；Cell B: SA:V = 18/9 = 2。Cell A 比率更高 → 扩散更高效，更依赖扩散。 |

## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Five features of gas exchange surface. [5]** — *State*
任五条（各 1 分）：
- ✓ **Large surface area** **[1]**
- ✓ **Thin** (short diffusion distance) **[1]**
- ✓ **Moist / wet surface** (gases dissolve before diffusing) **[1]**
- ✓ **Good blood supply** / maintained concentration gradient **[1]**
- ✓ **Ventilation** (medium renewed to maintain gradient) **[1]**

**(b) How alveolus is adapted. [6]** — *Explain（结构→功能）*
任三组结构-功能（各 2 分）：
- ✓ **Squamous epithelium / one cell thick wall** → very short diffusion distance / fast diffusion. **[2]**
- ✓ **Large number of alveoli** → very large total surface area → high rate of diffusion. **[2]**
- ✓ **Dense capillary network** → maintains steep O₂/CO₂ concentration gradient → continuous diffusion. **[2]**
- ✓ **Moist surface** → gases dissolve and can diffuse. **[2]**
- ✓ **Surfactant** → reduces surface tension → prevents alveolar collapse → maintains SA for exchange. **[2]**

### B2
**(a) Sequence of inspiration. [4]** — *Describe（因果链）*
- ✓ 1. Diaphragm **contracts** and flattens / moves downward. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. External intercostal muscles **contract** → ribcage moves up and out. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. **Thoracic / lung volume increases**. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. **Pressure in lungs falls below atmospheric** → air flows in. **[1]**
- ⚠️ 易错：说"肌肉把空气推进"——必须是"压力差驱动空气流入"。

**(b) Why quiet expiration is passive. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Diaphragm and external intercostal muscles **relax**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Lungs **recoil elastically** (due to elastic fibres), decreasing volume without muscular effort. **[1]**

**(c) What 4.2 dm³ represents. [1]**
- ✓ This is the **vital capacity** (VC) — maximum volume exhaled after a maximum inhalation. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Fish gill structure for gas exchange. [3]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Gill **lamellae (secondary lamellae)** provide a **large surface area**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Lamellae have walls one cell thick / thin → **short diffusion distance**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Dense **capillary network** within lamellae → maintains concentration gradient. **[1]**

**(b) Why countercurrent flow is more efficient than parallel flow. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. In countercurrent flow, blood and water flow in **opposite directions**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. A **diffusion gradient is maintained along the entire length** of the gill — water always has higher pO₂ than blood at every point. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. In parallel flow, O₂ levels **equalise** midway and diffusion stops — only ~50% efficiency vs ~80–90% for countercurrent. **[1]**

---

### B4
**(a) How O₂ reaches muscle cell in insect. [3]** — *Describe（路径）*
- ✓ 1. O₂ enters through **spiracles** on the body surface. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Diffuses along **trachea** (supported by chitin rings) and into **tracheoles**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Diffuses from the **moist ends of tracheoles** directly into muscle cells. **[1]**

**(b) Why insects are small due to gas exchange system. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. O₂ reaches cells by **diffusion through tracheoles** — diffusion is only efficient over **short distances**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. In a large body, inner cells would be **too far from tracheoles** to receive adequate O₂ by diffusion alone. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Percentage of O₂ extracted. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ 1. O₂ removed = 0.9 − 0.15 = 0.75 cm³ per 100 cm³. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. % extracted = 0.75 / 0.9 × 100 = **83.3%**. **[1]**

**(b) Data supporting countercurrent exchange. [3]** — *Explain（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. Blood entering gills has pO₂ of **0.2** and water leaving has **0.15** — blood is still lower than exiting water, consistent with full-length gradient. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Blood leaving has O₂ content of **0.8**, almost equal to entering water (**0.9**) — extremely efficient extraction. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. This high efficiency (83.3%) is only possible if a **concentration gradient is maintained throughout** the gill — evidence for countercurrent. **[1]**

**(c) Predicted O₂ in blood with parallel flow. [2]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. O₂ content of blood leaving would be **lower** (approximately 0.5–0.55 cm³/100 cm³, roughly equal to the midpoint). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. In parallel flow, concentrations **equilibrate** midway, so diffusion effectively stops and final blood O₂ ≈ average of entering blood and water = ~(0.2 + 0.9)/2. **[1]**

### C2
**(a) Calculate ventilation rate. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ At rest: 0.5 × 12 = **6.0 dm³ min⁻¹**. **[1]**
- ✓ Exercise: 1.8 × 24 = **43.2 dm³ min⁻¹**. **[1]**

**(b) Why TV and breathing rate increase during exercise. [3]** — *Describe + Explain*
- ✓ 1. Muscles respire more rapidly, producing **more CO₂** → blood pH falls. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Chemoreceptors detect **increased CO₂ / lower pH** and send signals to the **respiratory centre in the medulla**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Respiratory centre sends **more frequent / stronger signals** to breathing muscles → both TV and rate increase. **[1]**

**(c) Downward drift + calculation. [3]**
- ✓ 1. CO₂ absorber removes all CO₂ → only O₂ consumed changes volume → trace drifts **downward** as O₂ is used up. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Ventilation rate at rest = 6.0 dm³ min⁻¹; over 5 min = 6.0 × 5 = **30 dm³** of air breathed. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Tidal volume 0.5 dm³ × 12 per min × 5 min = 30 dm³ of air; O₂ content of air ~21%; O₂ consumed is a fraction — but the **baseline drop** on the spirometer directly shows O₂ consumed = amount the trace baseline has fallen. **[1]**
- 🎯 Spirometer 题：baseline 下降量 = O₂ 消耗量（单位 dm³），不需要计算通气量。

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Independent and dependent variables. [2]**
- ✓ Independent: **exercise intensity** (e.g. speed on treadmill / pedalling rate). **[1]**
- ✓ Dependent: **ventilation rate** (tidal volume × breathing rate, dm³ min⁻¹). **[1]**

**(b) Two variables to control. [2]**
- ✓ Any two: **same subject** each time ／ **same rest period** before measurement ／ **same temperature of environment** ／ duration of exercise before measuring. **[2]**

**(c) Safety precaution. [1]**
- ✓ Any one: ensure **subject is medically fit** before exercise ／ **sterilise mouthpiece** between subjects ／ ensure a **one-way valve** is used so subject doesn't rebreathe CO₂-rich air ／ monitor subject throughout for distress. **[1]**

**(d) Why baseline drops faster during exercise. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. During exercise, muscles respire at a **higher rate** → more O₂ consumed per minute. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. With CO₂ absorber, the increased O₂ consumption means **more gas volume removed** from the spirometer per unit time → baseline falls faster. **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
- B2(a) 最常丢分：断链——说"肌肉收缩→空气进入"跳过"容积↑→压力↓"步骤。要求学生写出完整四步因果链。
- C1(b) 数据题必须引用具体数值（如 0.2, 0.8, 0.9）而不只说"高/低"。
- B3(b) 逆流解释：强调"在出口端，血液 pO₂ 仍低于水 pO₂"——这是逆流比同向流高效的核心。
- 全题策略：气体交换题大多是 explain 型，需要写出 **因果链**，不能只列清单。
