## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which feature of xylem vessels allows water to move freely along them?
- A. They contain living cytoplasm
- B. They have no end walls between adjacent cells
- C. They are surrounded by companion cells
- D. Their walls contain cellulose microfibrils

**2.** Which pathway for water movement across the root cortex does NOT involve crossing a plasma membrane?
- A. Vacuolar pathway
- B. Symplast pathway
- C. Apoplast pathway
- D. Transcellular pathway

**3.** What is the role of the Casparian strip in the endodermis?
- A. It provides mechanical support to the root
- B. It forces water to enter the cytoplasm before reaching the xylem
- C. It actively pumps mineral ions into the xylem
- D. It controls the rate of transpiration from leaves

**4.** Which sequence correctly describes the cohesion-tension mechanism?
- A. Evaporation from mesophyll → tension in xylem → water pulled up → cohesion maintains column
- B. Root pressure pushes water → cohesion keeps column together → evaporation from leaf
- C. Active transport at root hair → water potential gradient → osmosis through xylem
- D. Tension from phloem → water moves to xylem → evaporation from stomata

**5.** Which condition would INCREASE the rate of transpiration?
- A. Increased humidity around the leaf
- B. Decreased air temperature
- C. Increased wind speed around the leaf
- D. Closed stomata

**6.** A potometer is used to measure:
- A. The actual rate of transpiration from a leaf
- B. The rate of water uptake by a leafy shoot (as an estimate of transpiration)
- C. The water potential of xylem sap
- D. The rate of photosynthesis in a leaf

**7.** Which structural feature is NOT an adaptation of a xerophyte?
- A. Sunken stomata in pits
- B. Thick, waxy cuticle
- C. Large, thin leaves to maximise surface area
- D. Rolled leaves trapping humid air

**8.** In the mass flow hypothesis for phloem transport, what creates the pressure gradient that drives movement?
- A. Active transport of water at source and sink
- B. High water potential at source and low water potential at sink
- C. High hydrostatic pressure at source (loading) and low pressure at sink (unloading)
- D. Cohesion of sucrose molecules in the sieve tube

**9.** Which cells provide ATP for the active loading of sucrose into phloem sieve tubes?
- A. Mesophyll cells
- B. Guard cells
- C. Companion cells
- D. Xylem vessel elements

**10.** A plant wilts in dry conditions. Which change in leaf water potential would you expect?
- A. Water potential becomes less negative (increases toward zero)
- B. Water potential becomes more negative (decreases below normal)
- C. Water potential remains the same as solute concentration does not change
- D. Water potential increases because stomata close

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** A scientist used aphids (a type of insect) to study phloem transport. Aphids pierce phloem sieve tubes with their mouthparts and feed on phloem sap. When the mouthpart was cut and left in place, phloem sap continued to flow out for hours.

   (a) Explain how this observation provides evidence for the mass flow hypothesis. **[2]**

   (b) Aphids were found in greater numbers near leaves (source) than near roots (sink). Suggest an explanation. **[2]**

   (c) The concentration of sucrose in phloem sap near a leaf was 0.30 mol dm⁻³. Near a root tip it was 0.05 mol dm⁻³. Calculate the percentage decrease in sucrose concentration from source to sink. Show your working. **[2]**

**2.** The table below shows data from an experiment measuring transpiration rate under different conditions.

| Condition | Transpiration rate / cm³ h⁻¹ |
|---|---|
| Still air, 20°C, 60% RH | 1.2 |
| Moving air, 20°C, 60% RH | 2.8 |
| Still air, 30°C, 60% RH | 2.1 |
| Still air, 20°C, 40% RH | 1.9 |

   (a) Describe and explain the effect of moving air on transpiration rate. **[3]**

   (b) Describe and explain the effect of increased temperature on transpiration rate. **[3]**

   (c) Identify **one** limitation of using a potometer to measure the data in this table. **[1]**

**3.** A cactus (xerophyte) and a rainforest plant are grown in identical conditions of high humidity and abundant water.

   (a) Predict how the stomatal opening of the cactus would compare to the rainforest plant under these conditions. Explain your prediction. **[3]**

   (b) Suggest why cacti still have sunken stomata even when grown in humid conditions. **[2]**
