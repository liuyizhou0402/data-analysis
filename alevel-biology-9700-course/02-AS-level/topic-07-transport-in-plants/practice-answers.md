## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Describe the apoplast pathway. [3]** — *command word: Describe（描述路径）*
- ✓ 1. Water moves through **cell walls and intercellular spaces** (not through cytoplasm). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Water moves by **osmosis / diffusion** along a water potential gradient. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Path: root hair cell wall → cortex cell walls → endodermis (where pathway is blocked). **[1]**

**(b) Explain why apoplast cannot continue through endodermis. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The endodermis cells have a **Casparian strip** — a band of **suberin (waterproof material)** in their cell walls. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Suberin **blocks water from moving through the cell wall**, forcing water into the cytoplasm (symplast). **[1]**
- ⚠️ 易错：只说"卡氏带阻挡水分"——需说明是**木栓质/suberin**阻水，且迫使进入共质体。

**(c) Explain how mineral ions concentrate in xylem. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Mineral ions are **actively transported** into xylem vessels by endodermal cells (using ATP). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This creates a **lower water potential** in the xylem, drawing water in by osmosis, and ions accumulate. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Two structural features of xylem, each with function. [4]** — *State + Explain*
任选两组，每组结构+功能得 2 分：
- ✓ **No end walls (lignified perforations removed)** → water can flow continuously without resistance. **[2]**
- ✓ **Thick lignified walls** → withstand the negative pressure (tension) without collapsing. **[2]**
- ✓ **Pits in walls** → allow lateral movement of water between adjacent vessels. **[2]**
- ✓ **Dead cells (no living content)** → no resistance to water flow through the lumen. **[2]**

**(b) Explain how tension is generated. [3]** — *Explain（连锁推理）*
- ✓ 1. Water evaporates from mesophyll cell walls into air spaces, and out through stomata (transpiration). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This lowers the water potential of mesophyll cells, drawing water from xylem by osmosis. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Water molecules are held together by **cohesion (hydrogen bonds)**, transmitting tension up the column. **[1]**

**(c) Effect of air bubble breaking the column. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Air bubble breaks the continuous water column, so tension **cannot be transmitted** above the bubble. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Water transport above the bubble would **stop / be greatly reduced**; plant may wilt. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Structure of sieve tube element vs xylem vessel. [4]** — *Describe + Compare*
- ✓ Sieve tube element: **thin cellulose walls; sieve plates (perforated end walls); living but lacks nucleus, few organelles**. **[2]**
- ✓ Xylem vessel: **lignified (thick) walls; no end walls; dead (no cytoplasm)**. **[2]**
- ⚠️ 易错：筛管是**活细胞**（但无核），导管是**死细胞**——常混淆。

**(b) Why companion cells are necessary. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Sieve tube elements **lack a nucleus and few mitochondria**, so cannot generate ATP independently. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Companion cells have many mitochondria and supply **ATP for active loading of sucrose** into sieve tubes. **[1]**

**(c) Outline mass flow hypothesis. [4]** — *Outline（按顺序）*
- ✓ 1. At the **source** (e.g. leaf), sucrose is **actively loaded** into sieve tubes → high solute concentration. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Water enters by **osmosis** → **high hydrostatic pressure** at source end. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. At the **sink** (e.g. root/growing tissue), sucrose is **unloaded** → low solute concentration, water leaves. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. **Pressure gradient** (source high → sink low) drives mass flow of solution along sieve tubes. **[1]**

### B4
**(a) Three factors that increase transpiration rate — explain each. [6]** — *Explain*

*(i) Increased light intensity:*
- ✓ Light → stomata open wider (guard cells gain turgor) → larger pore → **more water vapour diffuses out**. **[2]**

*(ii) Increased temperature:*
- ✓ Higher temp → water molecules have **more kinetic energy** → evaporate faster from mesophyll. **[1]**
- ✓ Also increases **water vapour concentration inside leaf** relative to outside → steeper water potential gradient. **[1]**

*(iii) Increased wind speed:*
- ✓ Wind removes humid air from leaf surface → **maintains a steep water vapour concentration gradient** between leaf and air. **[2]**

**(b) Method to compare transpiration in still vs moving air. [3]** — *Describe（实验操作）*
- ✓ 1. Set up potometer; allow air bubble to form; measure **distance bubble moves in fixed time (e.g. 5 min)** in still air. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Reset bubble; position a **fan at fixed distance and speed** to create moving air; repeat measurement. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Repeat each condition **multiple times** and calculate mean; **control temperature, light intensity, humidity**. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Aphid evidence for mass flow. [2]** — *Explain（引用实验证据）*
- ✓ 1. Sap flows continuously from the cut mouthpart **under pressure** — consistent with high hydrostatic pressure in phloem at source. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This supports mass flow: pressure drives bulk movement of solution, not just diffusion. **[1]**

**(b) More aphids near leaves than roots. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Leaves (sources) have **higher sucrose concentration** in phloem than roots (sinks). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Aphids gain more nutrients near source where sap is more concentrated / **pressure is higher** so flow is more accessible. **[1]**

**(c) Percentage decrease in sucrose concentration. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ 1. Decrease = (0.30 − 0.05) / 0.30 × 100. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. = 0.25 / 0.30 × 100 = **83.3%**. **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Effect of moving air — describe and explain. [3]** — *Describe + Explain（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. Moving air **increases** transpiration rate from 1.2 to **2.8 cm³ h⁻¹** (more than doubled). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Moving air removes water vapour from the leaf surface, **reducing humidity** at the leaf surface. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. This maintains a **steeper water vapour concentration (water potential) gradient** between leaf interior and air, so water vapour diffuses out faster. **[1]**

**(b) Effect of increased temperature — describe and explain. [3]**
- ✓ 1. Increasing temperature from 20°C to 30°C increases rate from 1.2 to **2.1 cm³ h⁻¹**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Higher temperature gives water molecules **more kinetic energy**, so they evaporate more rapidly from mesophyll cell walls. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Also increases water vapour concentration inside the leaf → **steeper gradient** for diffusion out. **[1]**

**(c) One limitation of potometer for this data. [1]**
- ✓ 任一：Potometer measures **water uptake, not transpiration directly** (some water is used in photosynthesis) ／ difficult to achieve same humidity / temperature conditions repeatedly ／ **only one shoot** at a time, difficult to compare. **[1]**

---

### C3
**(a) Cactus stomatal opening compared to rainforest plant. [3]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. Cactus stomata would **open less / be open for shorter periods** even in humid conditions. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Cacti are adapted to conserve water — stomata typically open only at **night (CAM metabolism)** or very briefly. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. This is a **genetic / evolutionary adaptation**, not directly controlled by current humidity. **[1]**

**(b) Why sunken stomata even in humid conditions. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Sunken stomata is a **fixed anatomical feature** that cannot change within the plant's lifetime. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The adaptation evolved for **xeric environments** and is retained even when conditions are not dry. **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Identify variables. [4]**
- ✓ Independent variable: **temperature** (of the air surrounding the shoot). **[1]**
- ✓ Dependent variable: **rate of water uptake** (distance moved by air bubble per unit time / cm min⁻¹). **[1]**
- ✓ Controlled variables (any two): **light intensity; humidity; wind speed / air movement; leaf surface area; same plant species / shoot**. **[2]**

**(b) Why multiple readings are better. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Allows calculation of a **mean rate**, reducing the effect of random variation. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Allows detection of **anomalous results** / shows whether rate is steady or changing over time. **[1]**

**(c) Bubble moves back when placed in water. [1]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ When shoot is placed in water, water is **absorbed by the cut stem**, pushing bubble back up the capillary tube. **[1]**

**(d) Why potometer only estimates transpiration. [1]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ Any one: some water absorbed is used in **photosynthesis** (not transpired) ／ water may leak at joints of apparatus ／ **stomata may respond** to changed conditions during experiment. **[1]**

- 计算题 C1(c) 注意：百分比减少 = (大−小)/大×100，**以原值为分母**，不以小值为分母。
