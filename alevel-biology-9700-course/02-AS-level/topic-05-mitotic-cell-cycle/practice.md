## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1）

**1.** During which stage of the cell cycle does DNA replication occur?
- A. G1 phase
- B. S phase
- C. G2 phase
- D. M phase (mitosis)

**2.** A cell has 46 chromosomes just before mitosis begins. How many chromosomes will each daughter cell contain after mitosis?
- A. 23
- B. 46
- C. 92
- D. 48

**3.** Which event occurs during **anaphase** of mitosis?
- A. Chromosomes align at the equator of the cell
- B. The nuclear envelope breaks down
- C. Sister chromatids are pulled to opposite poles by spindle fibres
- D. New nuclear envelopes form around each set of chromosomes

**4.** A student counts cells in a root tip squash. She observes 200 cells in total; 15 are in prophase, 5 in metaphase, 8 in anaphase, and 7 in telophase. What is the mitotic index?
- A. 0.075
- B. 0.175
- C. 0.125
- D. 0.35

**5.** Which feature is present in **metaphase** but absent in **interphase**?
- A. Nuclear DNA
- B. Nucleolus
- C. Condensed chromosomes aligned on the equatorial plate
- D. Mitochondria

**6.** Which statement about the role of **centrioles** in animal cells is correct?
- A. Centrioles replicate DNA before cell division
- B. Centrioles organise the spindle fibres during mitosis
- C. Centrioles produce ATP for cell division
- D. Centrioles are found in plant cells

**7.** A tumour suppressor gene normally prevents cells from dividing too rapidly. A mutation inactivates this gene. Which most likely outcome?
- A. Cells undergo apoptosis (programmed cell death)
- B. The cell cycle is uncontrolled, leading to tumour formation
- C. DNA replication becomes more accurate
- D. Cells differentiate more rapidly

**8.** Which row correctly shows the number of DNA molecules and chromosomes at **metaphase** of mitosis in a human cell (2n = 46)?

| | DNA molecules | Chromosomes |
|---|---|---|
| A | 46 | 46 |
| B | 92 | 46 |
| C | 46 | 92 |
| D | 92 | 92 |

**9.** Which describes a **malignant** tumour compared to a **benign** tumour?
- A. Malignant tumours grow slowly; benign tumours grow rapidly
- B. Malignant tumours remain localised; benign tumours spread
- C. Malignant tumours can invade neighbouring tissues and metastasise
- D. Benign tumours are caused by mutations; malignant tumours are not

**10.** A student counts a cell at interphase for 18 hours out of a total cell cycle of 24 hours. What percentage of cells in a growing tissue would be expected to be in mitosis (M phase)?
- A. 25%
- B. 75%
- C. 18%
- D. 6%

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application & Data（强化 AO2）

**1.** A researcher treats onion root tip cells with colchicine, a drug that prevents spindle formation, and then counts the number of cells at each stage of mitosis.

| Stage | Control (no drug) | After colchicine treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Interphase | 155 | 143 |
| Prophase | 23 | 47 |
| Metaphase | 11 | 89 |
| Anaphase | 6 | 0 |
| Telophase | 5 | 0 |
| Total | 200 | 279 |

   (a) Calculate the mitotic index for the control group. Show your working. **[2]**
   (b) Describe and explain the difference in metaphase counts between control and treated cells. **[3]**
   (c) Explain why there are zero cells in anaphase and telophase after colchicine treatment. **[2]**
   (d) **Suggest** why the total number of cells increased after colchicine treatment. **[2]**

**2.** The graph below shows the incidence of lung cancer per 100 000 people over 50 years, alongside cigarette consumption per person per year.
   *(Both curves rise steeply from 1920–1960; lung cancer peaks ~20–25 years after cigarette consumption peaks.)*

   (a) Describe the relationship between cigarette consumption and lung cancer incidence shown in the data. **[3]**
   (b) Explain why there is a time lag between the peak of cigarette consumption and the peak of lung cancer incidence. **[2]**
