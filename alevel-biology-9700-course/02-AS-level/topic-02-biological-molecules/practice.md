## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1）

**1.** Which property of water is most important for its role as a **solvent** in cells?
- A. High specific heat capacity
- B. High latent heat of vaporisation
- C. Water molecules are polar / dipole
- D. Water molecules form hydrogen bonds with each other

**2.** Which correctly describes the difference between α-glucose and β-glucose?
- A. They have different molecular formulae
- B. The –OH group on carbon-1 is on different sides of the ring
- C. α-glucose contains more oxygen atoms
- D. β-glucose cannot form polymers

**3.** A student tests an unknown solution with Benedict's reagent and observes no colour change. She then hydrolyses the solution with acid, neutralises it, and re-tests with Benedict's reagent; a brick-red precipitate forms. What does this indicate?
- A. The solution contained reducing sugar
- B. The solution contained a polysaccharide
- C. The solution contained a non-reducing disaccharide
- D. The solution contained a lipid

**4.** Which bond is formed during the condensation reaction between two glucose molecules?
- A. Hydrogen bond
- B. Peptide bond
- C. Ester bond
- D. Glycosidic bond

**5.** Which structural feature of cellulose makes it suitable as a structural component of cell walls?
- A. α-1,4 glycosidic bonds forming a coiled helix
- B. α-1,6 branch points every 8–12 glucose units
- C. β-1,4 glycosidic bonds forming straight chains that hydrogen-bond into microfibrils
- D. Many short chains that can be rapidly synthesised and broken down

**6.** A polypeptide chain contains 80 amino acids. How many water molecules were released during its formation by condensation?
- A. 80
- B. 81
- C. 79
- D. 160

**7.** Which statement about a phospholipid is correct?
- A. It has three fatty acid chains attached to glycerol
- B. The phosphate head is hydrophobic
- C. It is formed by condensation reactions
- D. Phospholipids cannot form bilayers in aqueous solution

**8.** Which bonds maintain the **secondary** structure of a protein?
- A. Disulfide bonds and ionic bonds
- B. Hydrogen bonds only
- C. Peptide bonds
- D. Hydrophobic interactions

**9.** A student carries out the Biuret test on two solutions. Solution X turns purple; Solution Y remains blue. Which conclusion is correct?
- A. Solution X contains reducing sugar; Solution Y does not
- B. Solution X contains protein; Solution Y does not
- C. Solution Y contains starch; Solution X does not
- D. Solution Y contains lipid; Solution X does not

**10.** Glycogen and amylopectin are both branched polysaccharides made of α-glucose. Which statement correctly compares them?
- A. Glycogen has fewer branch points than amylopectin
- B. Glycogen is found only in plant cells
- C. Glycogen is more highly branched than amylopectin, allowing faster enzyme access
- D. Amylopectin has α-1,6 linkages but glycogen does not
