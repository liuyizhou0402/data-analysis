## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Describe fluid mosaic model. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ **Phospholipid bilayer** — hydrophilic heads face outward, hydrophobic tails face inward. **[1]**
- ✓ **Proteins** (intrinsic/integral and extrinsic/peripheral) embedded in or on the surface of the bilayer. **[1]**
- ✓ **Cholesterol** molecules interspersed among phospholipid tails, regulating fluidity. **[1]**
- ✓ **Glycoproteins / glycolipids** on outer surface for cell recognition and signalling. **[1]**

**(b) Phospholipid bilayer as selective barrier. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ **Small, non-polar / lipid-soluble** molecules (e.g. O₂, CO₂, steroids) can diffuse freely through the hydrophobic core. **[1]**
- ✓ **Large, polar or charged** molecules (e.g. glucose, ions) cannot pass through — require **channel or carrier proteins**. **[1]**

### B2
**(a) Define osmosis. [2]** — *Define*
- ✓ The net movement of **water molecules**. **[1]**
- ✓ From a region of **higher water potential** to lower water potential, **through a partially permeable membrane**. **[1]**

**(b)(i) Calculate water potential. [1]**
- ✓ Ψ = Ψs + Ψp = −900 + 200 = **−700 kPa**. **[1]**

**(b)(ii) Net water movement. [2]** — *State and Explain*
- ✓ Water moves **into** the cell (net movement from solution into cell). **[1]**
- ✓ Cell Ψ = −700 kPa; solution Ψ = −600 kPa; cell has **lower** water potential → water moves from higher (−600) to lower (−700) water potential. **[1]**
- ⚠️ 水势计算常见错误：Ψs 已经是负值，**直接相加**，不再写成 Ψ = Ψs − Ψp。

**(c) Incipient plasmolysis. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ The point at which the **cell membrane just begins to pull away from the cell wall**. **[1]**
- ✓ Pressure potential (Ψp) = **0**; the cell is neither gaining nor losing water. **[1]**

### B3
**(a) Compare simple diffusion and facilitated diffusion. [4]** — *Compare（成对）*
- ✓ Simple diffusion occurs **directly through the phospholipid bilayer**; facilitated diffusion occurs **through channel or carrier proteins**.
- ✓ Simple diffusion: **no protein** required; facilitated diffusion: **requires specific membrane proteins**.
- ✓ Both are **passive** (no ATP) and move substances **down their concentration gradient**.
- ✓ Facilitated diffusion is **faster for large/polar molecules**; simple diffusion is limited to **small/non-polar** molecules.

**(b) Why large polar molecules cannot cross by simple diffusion. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ The **hydrophobic core** of the phospholipid bilayer repels polar/charged molecules. **[1]**
- ✓ Large molecules cannot pass through the **narrow hydrophobic region** between the tails. **[1]**

### B4
**(a) Co-transport mechanism. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase pump **actively transports Na⁺ out** of epithelial cells using ATP → low [Na⁺] inside cell. **[1]**
- ✓ This creates a **Na⁺ concentration gradient** (high outside, low inside). **[1]**
- ✓ Na⁺ moves down its gradient **into the cell via the co-transporter**, carrying glucose **against** its gradient. **[1]**
- ✓ Glucose accumulates inside cell and is transported into the blood by **facilitated diffusion**. **[1]**

**(b) Why absorption stops if ATP synthesis inhibited. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ Without ATP, the Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase pump **cannot function**. **[1]**
- ✓ Na⁺ is no longer pumped out → **no Na⁺ gradient** maintained → co-transporter cannot drive glucose uptake. **[1]**

### B5
**(a) Phagocytosis vs pinocytosis. [2]** — *Distinguish（成对）*
- ✓ **Phagocytosis** engulfs **large solid particles** (e.g. bacteria); **pinocytosis** takes in **small droplets of liquid / dissolved substances**. **[1+1]**

**(b) Exocytosis. [3]** — *Describe（步骤）*
- ✓ A **secretory vesicle** containing material moves towards the cell surface membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ The vesicle membrane **fuses** with the cell surface membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ The contents are **released to the outside** of the cell. **[1]**

## Part C — 数据题

### C1
**(a) Describe and explain 0.0–0.2 mol dm⁻³. [4]** — *Describe and Explain*
- ✓ Percentage change **decreases** from +8.2% to +1.0% as concentration increases from 0 to 0.2. **[1]** ← Describe + 引数据
- ✓ In distilled water/dilute solution, water potential of solution **> cell water potential**. **[1]** ← Explain
- ✓ Water enters cells by osmosis → cells become more **turgid** → tissue elongates. **[1]**
- ✓ As concentration increases, the water potential difference **decreases** → less water uptake → smaller increase. **[1]**

**(b) Estimate water potential of potato. [2]**
- ✓ The water potential of the potato ≈ the concentration at which **no change in length** occurs (0% change). **[1]**
- ✓ By interpolation between 0.2 (+1.0) and 0.3 (−2.1), the isotonic point is approximately **0.23 mol dm⁻³**; convert to water potential using known values or state this concentration as the estimate. **[1]**

**(c) Why percentage change rather than actual change. [1]**
- ✓ Cylinders may not have been exactly the **same initial length** → percentage accounts for any initial variation. **[1]**

**(d) Suggest why 0.5 showed smaller decrease than expected. [1]**
- ✓ At high concentrations, cells may be **fully plasmolysed** — once all water is lost, no further shrinkage is possible. **[1]**

### C2
**(a) Describe trend. [3]** — *Describe + 引数据 + 三步法*
- ✓ Between 0–40 °C, absorbance **increases only slightly** from 0.04 to 0.12. **[1]**
- ✓ Between 40–60 °C, absorbance **increases steeply** from 0.12 to 0.71. **[1]**
- ✓ Above 60 °C, absorbance **plateaus** at approximately 0.72 (no further increase). **[1]**

**(b) Explain 50–70 °C in terms of membrane structure. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ High temperatures **increase fluidity** of the phospholipid bilayer — phospholipid tails move more. **[1]**
- ✓ Above ~50 °C, **proteins denatured** / **gaps form** between phospholipids → membrane more permeable. **[1]**
- ✓ Red **betalain pigment** leaks from vacuole through tonoplast and cell membrane into surrounding water → higher absorbance. **[1]**

**(c) Absorbance does not increase above 60 °C. [1]**
- ✓ All cells are fully **disrupted / all betalain has leaked out** — no more pigment available to diffuse out. **[1]**

**(d) Colour of filter + explanation. [2]**
- ✓ **Green** filter (or orange filter). **[1]**
- ✓ Betalain is **red/purple** — it absorbs **green** light most strongly, so a green filter maximises the absorbance reading / gives greatest sensitivity. **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Identify incipient plasmolysis. [3]**
- ✓ Mount cells on a slide; observe under microscope at ×400. **[1]**
- ✓ Look for the concentration at which **50% of cells show the cell membrane just beginning to pull away** from the cell wall. **[1]**
- ✓ At incipient plasmolysis, Ψp = 0 and the protoplast is just detached / cell contents just shrink away from cell wall at corners. **[1]**

**(b) Range of concentrations. [2]**
- ✓ Allows determination of the **exact concentration** at which incipient plasmolysis occurs by **interpolation**. **[1]**
- ✓ A single concentration only gives a result of "plasmolysed" or "not plasmolysed" — cannot pinpoint the Ψ of the cell. **[1]**

**(c) Source of error and reduction. [2]**
- ✓ Error: cells may not all be the **same initial size / age / from the same part of the onion** — variability in water potential. **[1]**
- ✓ Reduction: use cells from the **same layer of the same onion**; use multiple cells at each concentration and take a mean. **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
1. **水势公式符号错误**：Ψs 已含负号，**直接相加**（Ψ = −900 + 200 = −700），不要写 −900 − 200。
2. **胆固醇功能表达不完整**：必须覆盖**高温稳定（防过流）AND 低温防固化**两个方向，缺一不给满分。
3. **数据题不引用数值**：C1a、C2a 都需引用表格/图中具体数据，不引数值自动丢第二个采分点。
