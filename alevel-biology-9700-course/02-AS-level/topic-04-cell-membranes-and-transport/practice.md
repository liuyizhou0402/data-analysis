## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1）

**1.** Which component of the cell surface membrane is responsible for cell signalling and cell recognition?
- A. Phospholipid molecules
- B. Cholesterol
- C. Glycoproteins and glycolipids
- D. Intrinsic proteins

**2.** What is the role of cholesterol in the cell membrane?
- A. To transport molecules across the membrane
- B. To regulate membrane fluidity — stabilising at high temperatures and preventing solidification at low temperatures
- C. To provide energy for active transport
- D. To act as a receptor for hormones only

**3.** A cell is placed in a solution with a lower water potential than the cell's cytoplasm. Which term describes the resulting state of an animal cell?
- A. Turgid
- B. Plasmolysed
- C. Crenated
- D. Flaccid

**4.** Which process requires a carrier protein but does **not** require ATP?
- A. Osmosis
- B. Active transport
- C. Facilitated diffusion
- D. Simple diffusion

**5.** The water potential (Ψ) of a cell is −650 kPa. The solute potential (Ψs) is −800 kPa. What is the pressure potential (Ψp)?
- A. −1450 kPa
- B. −150 kPa
- C. +150 kPa
- D. +1450 kPa

**6.** A plant cell is placed in a solution of water potential −1200 kPa. The cell has Ψs = −1000 kPa and Ψp = 0 kPa. Which statement is correct?
- A. Water enters the cell by osmosis
- B. Water leaves the cell by osmosis
- C. No net movement of water occurs
- D. The cell becomes turgid

**7.** Glucose is absorbed from the small intestine into epithelial cells against its concentration gradient using a sodium-glucose co-transporter. Which describes this process?
- A. Simple diffusion
- B. Facilitated diffusion
- C. Active transport
- D. Co-transport driven by the Na⁺ concentration gradient

**8.** Which is a correct difference between endocytosis and exocytosis?
- A. Endocytosis uses ATP; exocytosis does not
- B. Endocytosis brings material into the cell; exocytosis releases material from the cell
- C. Only exocytosis involves vesicle formation
- D. Endocytosis requires carrier proteins in the membrane

**9.** A student measures the mass of potato tissue before and after immersing it in solutions of different concentrations. In which solution would the potato gain the most mass?
- A. 1.0 mol dm⁻³ sucrose solution
- B. 0.5 mol dm⁻³ sucrose solution
- C. Distilled water
- D. 0.1 mol dm⁻³ sucrose solution

**10.** Which feature makes the rate of diffusion across a membrane **faster**?
- A. A thicker membrane
- B. A steeper concentration gradient
- C. A smaller surface area
- D. A lower temperature

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application & Data（强化 AO2）

**1.** A student cuts potato cylinders of equal length and places them in sucrose solutions of different concentrations. After 24 hours, she records the percentage change in length.

| Sucrose concentration / mol dm⁻³ | % change in length |
|---|---|
| 0.0 (distilled water) | +8.2 |
| 0.1 | +4.5 |
| 0.2 | +1.0 |
| 0.3 | −2.1 |
| 0.4 | −5.8 |
| 0.5 | −9.3 |

   (a) Describe and explain the results for concentrations 0.0 to 0.2 mol dm⁻³. **[4]**
   (b) Use the data to estimate the water potential of the potato tissue. Explain your reasoning. **[2]**
   (c) Explain why the student used **percentage change** in length rather than actual change. **[1]**
   (d) **Suggest** why cylinders in 0.5 mol dm⁻³ showed a smaller percentage decrease than expected compared to 0.4 mol dm⁻³. **[1]**

**2.** A student investigates the effect of temperature on the permeability of beetroot cell membranes. She cuts discs of beetroot and places them in distilled water at temperatures from 0 °C to 70 °C for 30 minutes. She then measures the absorbance of the water using a colorimeter.

| Temperature / °C | Absorbance |
|---|---|
| 0 | 0.04 |
| 10 | 0.05 |
| 20 | 0.06 |
| 30 | 0.07 |
| 40 | 0.12 |
| 50 | 0.38 |
| 60 | 0.71 |
| 70 | 0.72 |

   (a) Describe the trend in the data. **[3]**
   (b) Explain the results at 50–70 °C in terms of membrane structure. **[3]**
   (c) Explain why absorbance does not increase further above 60 °C. **[1]**
