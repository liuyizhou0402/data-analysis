## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which statement about phagocytosis is correct?
- A. Phagocytosis is a specific immune response that recognises particular antigens
- B. Phagocytes engulf pathogens and digest them using lysosomal enzymes
- C. Phagocytes produce antibodies after digesting a pathogen
- D. Phagocytosis only occurs in the lymph nodes

**2.** Which cells produce antibodies?
- A. T helper cells
- B. Memory B cells
- C. Plasma cells
- D. Cytotoxic T cells

**3.** A patient receives an injection of antibodies against tetanus toxin immediately after being wounded with a rusty nail. Which type of immunity does this represent?
- A. Natural active immunity
- B. Artificial active immunity
- C. Natural passive immunity
- D. Artificial passive immunity

**4.** Why does a second exposure to the same antigen produce a faster and stronger antibody response?
- A. More B cells are produced during the second exposure
- B. Memory cells rapidly proliferate and differentiate when re-exposed to the specific antigen
- C. The pathogen produces less antigen during the second infection
- D. Antibodies from the first infection remain in the blood

**5.** In the production of monoclonal antibodies, why are myeloma cells used in the hybridoma process?
- A. They produce the antigen needed for immunisation
- B. They provide the ability to divide indefinitely in culture
- C. They secrete large quantities of antibodies
- D. They produce the specific antibody required

**6.** Which statement about the structure of an antibody is correct?
- A. Antibodies consist of two identical polypeptide chains
- B. The constant region of the antibody binds specifically to the antigen
- C. Each antibody has two antigen-binding sites formed by the variable regions
- D. Antibodies are lipoproteins secreted by T cells

**7.** What is meant by 'clonal selection'?
- A. All lymphocytes are produced from a single clone
- B. The lymphocyte with a complementary receptor to a specific antigen is activated and proliferates
- C. Identical antibodies are selected in the laboratory for use as monoclonal antibodies
- D. Memory cells are selected to produce secondary immune responses

**8.** HIV causes AIDS primarily by:
- A. Producing toxins that destroy red blood cells
- B. Directly infecting and destroying B cells, preventing antibody production
- C. Infecting and destroying T helper cells, weakening both humoral and cell-mediated immunity
- D. Blocking the production of lysosomal enzymes in phagocytes

   (b) The mAb was conjugated (attached) to a cytotoxic drug. Explain how this would improve its effectiveness as a cancer treatment. **[2]**
