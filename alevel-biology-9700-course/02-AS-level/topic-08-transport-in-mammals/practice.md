## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Why is the wall of the left ventricle thicker than that of the right ventricle?
- A. The left ventricle pumps blood a greater distance around the body
- B. The left ventricle contains more blood than the right ventricle
- C. The right ventricle only pumps blood to the lungs, which requires less pressure
- D. The left atrium is larger than the right atrium

**2.** During which phase of the cardiac cycle are the atrioventricular valves closed?
- A. Atrial systole only
- B. Ventricular diastole
- C. Ventricular systole
- D. Both atrial and ventricular systole

**3.** What is the role of the atrioventricular node (AVN) in the cardiac cycle?
- A. It generates the initial electrical impulse for each heartbeat
- B. It delays the impulse, allowing the ventricles to fill before contracting
- C. It controls the diameter of coronary arteries
- D. It prevents nerve signals from the brain reaching the heart

**4.** In which blood vessel would you expect to find blood with the highest concentration of oxygen?
- A. Pulmonary artery
- B. Vena cava
- C. Pulmonary vein
- D. Hepatic portal vein

**5.** Which statement correctly describes the Bohr effect?
- A. Increased CO₂ concentration increases the affinity of haemoglobin for oxygen
- B. Increased CO₂ concentration shifts the oxygen dissociation curve to the left
- C. Increased CO₂ concentration decreases haemoglobin's affinity for oxygen, releasing more O₂ to tissues
- D. Decreased pH increases haemoglobin's affinity for oxygen

**6.** Foetal haemoglobin (HbF) has a higher affinity for oxygen than adult haemoglobin (HbA). How is this represented on an oxygen dissociation curve?
- A. HbF curve is to the right of the HbA curve
- B. HbF curve is to the left of the HbA curve
- C. HbF curve has a lower maximum saturation than HbA
- D. HbF curve is linear rather than S-shaped

**7.** Which component of blood does NOT normally pass into tissue fluid?
- A. Glucose
- B. Water
- C. Plasma proteins
- D. Oxygen

**8.** A patient has a low concentration of plasma proteins due to liver disease. What is the most likely consequence?
- A. Higher blood pressure
- B. Oedema (swelling of tissues)
- C. Increased rate of tissue fluid reabsorption
- D. Decreased lymph production

**9.** Which row correctly describes the structure of capillaries compared to arteries?

| | Wall thickness | Lumen diameter | Valves |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Thicker | Smaller | Present |
| B | Thinner | Smaller | Absent |
| C | Thinner | Larger | Absent |
| D | Thicker | Larger | Present |

**10.** A student records an ECG. The time between two consecutive R-peaks is 0.75 s. What is the heart rate?
- A. 75 beats per minute
- B. 80 beats per minute
- C. 60 beats per minute
- D. 100 beats per minute
