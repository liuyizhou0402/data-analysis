## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Define infectious disease. [1]** — *Define*
- ✓ A disease **caused by a pathogen** that can be **transmitted from one individual to another**. **[1]**

**(b) Two pathogen types and diseases. [4]** — *Name*
任两组，各 2 分（类型 1 分 + 疾病 1 分）：
- ✓ **Virus** → e.g. AIDS / influenza / COVID-19. **[2]**
- ✓ **Bacterium** → e.g. tuberculosis / cholera. **[2]**
- ✓ **Fungus** → e.g. athlete's foot / candidiasis. **[2]**
- ✓ **Protist** → malaria. **[2]**

**(c) Define vector, using malaria example. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. A vector is an organism that **transmits a pathogen** from one host to another. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. For malaria, the female *Anopheles* mosquito **carries *Plasmodium* and injects it** into humans when it feeds. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Two transmission routes for HIV. [2]** — *Describe*
任两条：
- ✓ **Unprotected sexual intercourse** (transfer of infected body fluids). **[1]**
- ✓ **Sharing hypodermic needles** (contaminated blood). **[1]**
- ✓ **Blood transfusion** with infected blood. **[1]**
- ✓ **Mother to child** during birth or breastfeeding. **[1]**

**(b) How HIV causes immune failure. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. HIV **infects T helper (CD4⁺) cells** of the immune system. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. HIV replicates inside T helper cells, eventually **destroying them**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. As T helper cell numbers fall, the immune response is weakened — **fewer antibodies produced** / cell-mediated immunity diminished → cannot fight pathogens. **[1]**

**(c) Why AIDS patient susceptible to fungal pneumonia. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. AIDS has destroyed most **T helper cells** → immune system severely compromised. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The patient cannot mount an **effective immune response** against the fungus, which normally a healthy immune system would suppress (**opportunistic infection**). **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Why streptomycin kills TB bacteria but not human cells. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. *M. tuberculosis* has **70S ribosomes** (prokaryotic), which streptomycin binds to. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Human cells have **80S ribosomes** (eukaryotic) which are not targeted by streptomycin → human cells unaffected. **[1]**

**(b) Why full 6-month antibiotic course must be completed. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Symptoms may disappear while **some bacteria remain**, hidden in granulomas or dormant. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Stopping early leaves **resistant / less susceptible bacteria** still alive. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. These surviving bacteria **reproduce and spread**, potentially causing a treatment-resistant relapse. **[1]**

**(c) How antibiotic resistance spreads in bacterial population. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. A **random mutation** in a bacterium confers resistance (e.g. alters ribosome structure). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Antibiotic kills sensitive bacteria, but **resistant individuals survive and reproduce** (natural selection). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Resistance gene can spread **vertically (to daughter cells)** and **horizontally (via plasmids)** to other bacteria. **[1]**

---

### B4
**(a) Why overcrowding increases TB transmission. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. More people in **close contact** in enclosed spaces → **airborne droplets** containing bacteria spread more easily. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Poor ventilation means bacteria **remain in the air longer** → higher chance of inhalation. **[1]**

**(b) Two other factors + explanations. [4]** — *Suggest + Explain*
任两组（各 2 分）：
- ✓ **Poor sanitation / lack of clean water** → pathogens in faeces contaminate water supply → **faecal-oral transmission** (e.g. cholera). **[2]**
- ✓ **Malnutrition / poor diet** → weakened immune system → **greater susceptibility** to infection and more severe disease. **[2]**
- ✓ **Low vaccination coverage** → no herd immunity → pathogens **spread more easily** through the population. **[2]**
- ✓ **International travel** → infected individuals **carry pathogens to new regions** where there is no pre-existing immunity. **[2]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Describe trend in malaria cases with data. [2]** — *Describe（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. Malaria cases **remained relatively constant** at approximately **200,000 per year** from Year 1 to Year 3. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. After the bed-net programme started in Year 4, cases **gradually declined** to approximately **40,000 by Year 8**. **[1]**

**(b) How bed nets reduce malaria. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Insecticide-treated nets **kill mosquitoes** that land on them. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Nets also form a **physical barrier** preventing mosquitoes from biting sleeping humans. **[1]**

**(c) Why cases didn't fall to zero. [2]** — *Suggest*
任两条：
- ✓ Not all people in the area received / **use bed nets consistently** (compliance issues). **[1]**
- ✓ **Mosquitoes can still bite** during daylight hours when people are not under nets. **[1]**
- ✓ Mosquitoes may have **developed resistance** to the insecticide. **[1]**
- ✓ *Plasmodium* can survive in **animal reservoirs** / non-human hosts. **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Two factors explaining higher MRSA in Country B. [2]** — *Suggest*
任两条：
- ✓ **Higher/more inappropriate use of antibiotics** in Country B (e.g. over-the-counter availability) → stronger selection pressure. **[1]**
- ✓ **Poorer infection control in hospitals** → greater hospital-to-community transmission of MRSA. **[1]**
- ✓ **Less rigorous antibiotic stewardship programmes** → methicillin used more broadly. **[1]**

**(b) How MRSA evolved by natural selection. [4]** — *Explain（进化机制）*
- ✓ 1. In the original *S. aureus* population, **random mutations** occurred that conferred resistance to methicillin. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. When methicillin was used in hospitals, **susceptible bacteria were killed** but resistant variants **survived**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Resistant bacteria **reproduced**, passing the resistance gene to **daughter cells** (vertical transmission). **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Over time, the proportion of **resistant bacteria increased**; methicillin became ineffective against this population. **[1]**

**(c) Stopping methicillin use → reduced MRSA. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Without antibiotic selection pressure, **resistant bacteria have no advantage** over sensitive ones. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Sensitive bacteria may have a **competitive advantage** (resistance genes can have a metabolic cost), so their proportion increases over time. **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
- B3(c) 耐药性进化：必须包含"随机突变预先存在"（pre-existing mutation）——不能说"细菌为了适应而突变"（这是拉马克错误）。
- C2(b) 4 分题：依次写"突变存在 → 选择压力 → 存活繁殖 → 比例增大"四步，缺一不可。
- B2(b) HIV 机制：强调 T helper 细胞被**摧毁**（destroyed），不是被感染就没事——T 细胞数量下降才是关键。
- 全题：describe 题必须引用数字（题目有数据则必须引用），explain 题必须写出因果链。
