## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Define enzyme. [2]** — *Define（精准定义）*
- ✓ A **biological catalyst** (protein). **[1]**
- ✓ That **lowers the activation energy** of a reaction / speeds up a reaction without being used up. **[1]**
- ⚠️ 只说"catalyst"不得分，必须说**biological** / 蛋白质；必须写"lowers activation energy"不是"provides energy"。

**(b) Explain induced-fit catalysis. [3]** — *Explain（讲机制）*
- ✓ Substrate collides with and **binds to the active site** of the enzyme. **[1]**
- ✓ The active site **changes shape** to fit more closely around the substrate. **[1]**
- ✓ This **strains/destabilises bonds** in the substrate, lowering activation energy → products released, enzyme unchanged. **[1]**

**(c) Difference between lock-and-key and induced-fit. [1]** — *State*
- ✓ Lock-and-key: active site is a **rigid, pre-formed** complementary shape; induced-fit: active site is **flexible** and changes shape upon substrate binding. **[1]**

### B2
**(a) Describe and explain effect from 10–37 °C. [3]** — *Describe and Explain*
- ✓ Rate **increases** (from ~0.8 to ~3.2 μmol min⁻¹). **[1]** ← Describe（引数据）
- ✓ Higher temperature gives molecules (enzyme and substrate) **more kinetic energy**. **[1]** ← Explain
- ✓ → More frequent **successful collisions** between substrate and active site / enzyme-substrate complexes form more often. **[1]**

**(b) Explain fall to zero above 55 °C. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ High temperature causes **bonds (hydrogen bonds, ionic bonds)** maintaining the enzyme's tertiary structure to **break**. **[1]**
- ✓ The **shape of the active site changes** (distorted/permanently altered). **[1]**
- ✓ Substrate can no longer bind / enzyme is **denatured** → no enzyme-substrate complexes form. **[1]**
- ⚠️ 必须说"active site shape changes"而非只说"enzyme breaks down"。

**(c) Predict rate after cooling from 70 °C. [2]** — *Predict and Explain*
- ✓ Rate would remain **at zero / very low** even after cooling. **[1]**
- ✓ Because denaturation is **irreversible** — the bonds cannot reform in the same configuration. **[1]**

### B3
**(a) Compare competitive vs non-competitive inhibition. [4]** — *Compare（成对）*
- ✓ Competitive inhibitor binds to the **active site**; non-competitive binds to an **allosteric site**.
- ✓ Competitive inhibitor is **structurally similar to substrate**; non-competitive is structurally different.
- ✓ Effect of competitive inhibitor can be **reduced by increasing substrate concentration**; non-competitive **cannot** be overcome by excess substrate.
- ✓ Competitive inhibition: **Vmax unchanged, Km increases**; non-competitive: **Vmax decreases, Km unchanged**.

**(b) Why excess substrate reduces drug effectiveness. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ Drug (competitive inhibitor) and natural substrate **compete for the same active site**. **[1]**
- ✓ Higher substrate concentration → more substrate molecules **outcompete** the drug → less inhibition of enzyme. **[1]**

### B4
**(a) Two advantages of immobilised urease. [2]** — *State*
- ✓ Can be **recovered and reused** after the reaction, reducing costs. **[1]**
- ✓ Product is not **contaminated** with enzyme (easier to purify product). **[1]**
- (Also accept: more stable / suitable for continuous flow processes)

**(b) Km of immobilised urease may differ. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ Immobilisation may **change the shape of the active site slightly** / restrict flexibility of enzyme. **[1]**
- ✓ The substrate may have **restricted access** to the active site when enzyme is embedded in alginate. **[1]**

### B5
**(a) Km of enzyme. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ The substrate concentration at which the reaction rate is **half** the maximum rate (½ Vmax). **[1]**
- ✓ It is a measure of the **affinity** of the enzyme for its substrate. **[1]**

**(b) Enzyme A (Km 2) vs Enzyme B (Km 8). [2]** — *State and Explain*
- ✓ Enzyme **A** has higher affinity. **[1]**
- ✓ Because a **lower Km** means less substrate is needed to achieve ½ Vmax → enzyme binds substrate more readily / tighter binding. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1 — 数据题（三步法：Describe–Quote–Explain）
**(a) Describe trend 10–40 °C. [2]** — *Describe（引数据）*
- ✓ Rate **increases** as temperature rises from 10 to 40 °C. **[1]**
- ✓ Rate increases from **0.8 to 3.2 μmol min⁻¹** (a 4-fold increase). **[1]**
- ⚠️ 不引用具体数值（0.8/3.2）直接丢 1 分。

**(b) Explain results 40–60 °C. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ Between 40–50 °C rate **decreases** from 3.2 to 1.1 μmol min⁻¹. **[1]** ← Describe
- ✓ Above optimum temperature, thermal energy breaks **hydrogen bonds** maintaining active site shape. **[1]**
- ✓ At 60 °C, enzyme is fully **denatured** → active site permanently changed → no substrate binding → rate = 0. **[1]**

**(c) Percentage decrease 40→50 °C. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ (3.2 − 1.1) / 3.2 × 100. **[1]**
- ✓ = **65.6%** (accept 66%). **[1]**

**(d) Water bath vs Bunsen. [1]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ Water bath provides more **uniform / stable** temperature control; Bunsen burner causes **fluctuations**. **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Variables. [4]** — *Identify*
- ✓ Independent variable: **pH** (controlled by buffer solutions). **[1]**
- ✓ Dependent variable: **volume of O₂ produced in 3 minutes** (measured with gas syringe / inverted burette). **[1]**
- ✓ Controlled variable 1: **temperature** (water bath at constant temperature). **[1]**
- ✓ Controlled variable 2: **concentration/volume of H₂O₂** / enzyme concentration. **[1]**

**(b) Why use buffer solution. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ Buffer maintains a **constant / known pH** throughout the experiment. **[1]**
- ✓ Without buffer, the reaction itself or CO₂ production could change the pH, **confounding the results**. **[1]**

**(c) Determine denatured vs inhibited at pH 3. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ After exposing enzyme to pH 3, **return pH to optimum** (e.g. pH 7) by adding buffer. **[1]**
- ✓ If activity **recovers** → enzyme was inhibited (not denatured); if still no activity → enzyme was **denatured** (irreversible). **[1]**

**(d) Sketch expected graph. [2]** — *Sketch and Describe*
- ✓ Bell-shaped (inverted U) curve with maximum at approximately **pH 7–8** (optimum for catalase). **[1]**
- ✓ Activity drops steeply on both sides of the optimum; approaches zero at pH < 3 and pH > 11. **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
1. **Compare 未成对**：B3a 要求对比两种抑制剂，大量学生只描述一种，不给另一种对应信息——每组须成对，各 1 分。
2. **数据题未引用数值**：C1a 要求引用 0.8→3.2，不引数据不得第二个采分点。每节课后反复强调"说趋势、引数据"。
3. **Explain 变性链条不完整**：正确链条须有三步：高温 → 氢键/键断裂 → 活性位点形状改变 → 底物无法结合；只说"蛋白质分解"或"活性位点破坏"不足。
