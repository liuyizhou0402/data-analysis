## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1）

**1.** Which statement correctly describes the **induced-fit model** of enzyme action?
- A. The active site is a rigid, complementary shape to the substrate
- B. The active site changes shape to fit more precisely around the substrate
- C. The substrate changes shape to fit the active site
- D. Enzyme and substrate form a permanent complex

**2.** An enzyme is heated to 80 °C. Its activity decreases to zero and does not recover when cooled. The most likely explanation is:
- A. The substrate concentration decreased
- B. The pH changed during heating
- C. The enzyme was denatured irreversibly
- D. The activation energy increased

**3.** Which graph shows the correct effect of increasing substrate concentration on reaction rate when enzyme concentration is kept constant?
- A. Linear increase with no plateau
- B. Decrease then increase
- C. Rapid increase then plateau as enzyme becomes saturated
- D. No change at any substrate concentration

**4.** A competitive inhibitor is added to an enzyme-catalysed reaction. Which statement about the effect on Vmax and Km is correct?
- A. Vmax decreases; Km is unchanged
- B. Vmax is unchanged; Km increases
- C. Both Vmax and Km decrease
- D. Both Vmax and Km increase

**5.** A non-competitive inhibitor binds to the **allosteric site** of an enzyme. How does this affect enzyme activity?
- A. It blocks the active site directly
- B. It changes the shape of the active site, reducing substrate binding
- C. It increases the activation energy only at high pH
- D. It prevents product release from the active site

**6.** What does a **low Km value** indicate about an enzyme?
- A. The enzyme requires a high substrate concentration to reach half-maximum velocity
- B. The enzyme has a high affinity for its substrate
- C. The enzyme is easily denatured
- D. The enzyme has a high maximum velocity

**7.** Immobilised enzymes are used in industry to produce lactose-free milk. Which is an advantage of using immobilised enzymes?
- A. They always work faster than free enzymes
- B. They can be recovered and reused, reducing costs
- C. They are not affected by temperature or pH
- D. They require no substrate to function

**8.** A student investigates the effect of pH on amylase activity. She measures the time for starch to be completely digested. At pH 7 the time is 4 minutes; at pH 3 the time is 24 minutes. What can be concluded?
- A. pH 3 is the optimum for amylase
- B. Amylase is denatured at pH 7
- C. Amylase is more active at pH 7 than at pH 3
- D. The substrate concentration was too low at pH 3

**9.** Which correctly describes the effect of an enzyme on a reaction?
- A. It increases the overall energy change (ΔG) of the reaction
- B. It lowers the activation energy
- C. It provides energy to start the reaction
- D. It changes the products of the reaction

**10.** Two enzyme inhibitors X and Y are tested. Adding excess substrate overcomes the inhibition by X but not by Y. What type of inhibitors are X and Y?

| | X | Y |
|---|---|---|
| A | Non-competitive | Competitive |
| B | Competitive | Non-competitive |
| C | Competitive | Competitive |
| D | Non-competitive | Non-competitive |
