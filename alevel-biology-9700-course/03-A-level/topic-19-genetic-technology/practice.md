## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** What is the function of a restriction enzyme in genetic engineering?
- A. To join DNA fragments together
- B. To cut DNA at specific recognition sequences
- C. To copy mRNA into cDNA
- D. To amplify DNA fragments

**2.** Why is the same restriction enzyme used to cut both the gene of interest and the plasmid vector?
- A. To ensure the DNA fragments are the same length
- B. To produce complementary sticky ends that can join together
- C. To prevent the plasmid from replicating
- D. To remove the introns from the gene

**3.** Why is cDNA, rather than genomic DNA, often used when inserting a eukaryotic gene into a bacterium?
- A. cDNA is shorter and easier to handle
- B. Bacteria cannot remove introns, and cDNA has no introns
- C. cDNA is more stable than genomic DNA
- D. Genomic DNA cannot be cut by restriction enzymes

**4.** During PCR, what happens during the denaturation step at about 95°C?
- A. Primers bind to the single-stranded DNA
- B. DNA polymerase synthesises new strands
- C. The hydrogen bonds between the two DNA strands break, separating them
- D. The DNA fragments are separated by size

**5.** Why is Taq polymerase used in PCR rather than the DNA polymerase found in human cells?
- A. Taq polymerase works faster
- B. Taq polymerase is heat-stable and is not denatured at high temperatures
- C. Taq polymerase does not require primers
- D. Taq polymerase can copy RNA

**6.** During gel electrophoresis, in which direction do DNA fragments move and why?
- A. Toward the negative electrode, because DNA is positively charged
- B. Toward the positive electrode, because DNA is negatively charged
- C. They do not move; they stay in the wells
- D. Toward the positive electrode, because DNA is neutral

**7.** In gel electrophoresis, which DNA fragments travel the furthest?
- A. The largest fragments
- B. The smallest fragments
- C. All fragments travel the same distance
- D. Only fragments with sticky ends

**8.** What is the purpose of a genetic marker in gene cloning?
- A. To cut the DNA at specific sites
- B. To identify cells that have successfully taken up the recombinant plasmid
- C. To amplify the gene of interest
- D. To join the gene to the plasmid

**9.** Which type of gene therapy involves changes that could be inherited by future generations?
- A. Somatic cell gene therapy
- B. Germ line gene therapy
- C. PCR-based therapy
- D. Marker gene therapy

**10.** Which enzyme is used to produce cDNA from mRNA?
- A. DNA ligase
- B. Restriction enzyme
- C. Reverse transcriptase
- D. Taq polymerase
