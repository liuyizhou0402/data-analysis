## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Obtain gene as cDNA. [3]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Extract **mRNA** for insulin from cells that express it (e.g. β cells). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Use **reverse transcriptase** to synthesise a complementary DNA (**cDNA**) strand from the mRNA. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. cDNA has **no introns**, so it can be expressed correctly in bacteria. **[1]**

**(b) Insert gene into plasmid. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Cut both the gene and the plasmid with the **same restriction enzyme**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This produces **complementary sticky ends** on both. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Mix gene and plasmid; the sticky ends **base-pair** by complementary base pairing. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. **DNA ligase** joins the sugar-phosphate backbones → recombinant plasmid. **[1]**

**(c) How genetic markers identify successful uptake. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The plasmid contains a **marker gene** (e.g. antibiotic resistance or fluorescence). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Bacteria are grown under conditions that reveal the marker (e.g. **with antibiotic / under UV light**). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Only bacteria that took up the plasmid **survive / fluoresce** → identifies transformed cells. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Three stages of PCR + temperatures. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. **Denaturation (~95°C)**: DNA strands separate as hydrogen bonds break. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **Annealing (~55°C)**: primers bind to complementary sequences on single strands. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. **Extension (~72°C)**: DNA polymerase synthesises new strands from the primers. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. The cycle is **repeated**, doubling the DNA each time. **[1]**

**(b) Why heat-stable polymerase essential. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The denaturation step reaches ~95°C, which would **denature an ordinary enzyme**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Taq polymerase is **heat-stable** (from a thermophilic bacterium), so it remains active through all cycles. **[1]**

**(c) Copies after 10 cycles. [1]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ 2¹⁰ = **1024 copies**. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Principle of gel electrophoresis. [4]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. DNA fragments are placed in wells in an agarose gel and an **electric field** is applied. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. DNA is **negatively charged** (phosphate groups), so it moves toward the **positive electrode (anode)**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. **Smaller fragments move faster/further** through the gel pores; larger fragments move more slowly. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Fragments are thus **separated by size**; their positions can be compared to a known size marker. **[1]**

**(b) One application. [2]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. **DNA profiling / fingerprinting** (e.g. forensic identification or paternity testing). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Comparing band patterns to identify or match individuals / detect a disease allele. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/评价题

### C1
**(a) Two advantages of GM bacteria for insulin. [2]** — *Suggest*
任两条：
- ✓ Produces insulin **identical to human insulin** → less likely to cause allergic/immune reactions than animal insulin. **[1]**
- ✓ Can be produced in **large quantities, cheaply and reliably** (no dependence on animal supply). **[1]**
- ✓ Avoids **ethical objections** of some groups to using animal products. **[1]**

**(b) Why gene must be expressed correctly. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The gene must be **transcribed and translated** correctly to produce the right amino acid sequence (primary structure). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Only the correct sequence folds into a **functional 3D structure** that works as insulin. **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Two advantages of Bt maize. [2]** — *Suggest*
任两条：
- ✓ **Higher yields** (less crop lost to insect pests). **[1]**
- ✓ **Reduced use of chemical pesticides** → lower cost and less environmental pollution. **[1]**

**(b) Two concerns about GM crops. [2]** — *Suggest*
任两条：
- ✓ **Gene flow** to wild relatives / other crops (e.g. herbicide-resistant "superweeds"). **[1]**
- ✓ Possible harm to **non-target organisms** (e.g. beneficial insects) / reduced biodiversity. **[1]**
- ✓ Pests may **evolve resistance** to the Bt toxin. **[1]**

**(c) Discuss whether benefits outweigh risks. [3]** — *Discuss（两面+结论）*
- ✓ 1. Benefits: higher yields, less pesticide, can address food security / malnutrition (e.g. golden rice). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Risks: gene flow, biodiversity loss, resistance evolution, ethical/regulatory concerns. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. **Balanced conclusion**: e.g. benefits may outweigh risks if properly regulated and monitored; depends on the crop and context. **[1]**

### C3
**(a) How DNA used to identify father. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The child's DNA profile is compared with the mother's and each potential father's bands. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Bands in the child not inherited from the mother must come from the **biological father**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The man whose bands **match these paternal bands** (Man 2) is the biological father. **[1]**

**(b) Why each child band matches a parent. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. A child inherits **one set of chromosomes (alleles) from each parent**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. So every band in the child's profile must originate from **either the mother or the father** (no new bands appear). **[1]**
