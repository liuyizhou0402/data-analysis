## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** The three-domain classification system is based primarily on differences in:
- A. Body size
- B. Ribosomal RNA (rRNA) sequences
- C. Habitat
- D. Mode of nutrition

**2.** Which is the correct order of taxonomic ranks, from largest to smallest?
- A. Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species
- B. Kingdom, Class, Phylum, Family, Order, Genus, Species
- C. Phylum, Kingdom, Class, Order, Genus, Family, Species
- D. Species, Genus, Family, Order, Class, Phylum, Kingdom

**3.** In the binomial name *Panthera leo*, what does "*Panthera*" represent?
- A. The species
- B. The genus
- C. The family
- D. The kingdom

**4.** Which of these best describes "species evenness"?
- A. The total number of species in a community
- B. The number of individuals of each species
- C. How similar in abundance the different species are
- D. The number of habitats in an ecosystem

**5.** Using Simpson's Index of Diversity (D = 1 − Σ(n/N)²), a value of D close to 1 indicates:
- A. Low biodiversity, dominated by one species
- B. High biodiversity with many evenly distributed species
- C. Only one species is present
- D. The ecosystem is unstable

**6.** Why is a random number generator used to position quadrats in random sampling?
- A. To save time during fieldwork
- B. To ensure quadrats are placed in the most diverse areas
- C. To avoid bias in the choice of sampling positions
- D. To make sure quadrats do not overlap

**7.** Which method would be most appropriate for estimating the population size of a mobile animal such as a woodlouse?
- A. Quadrat sampling
- B. A belt transect
- C. Mark-release-recapture
- D. Counting all individuals directly

**8.** Conservation of a species in a seed bank or a zoo is an example of:
- A. In situ conservation
- B. Ex situ conservation
- C. Sustainable management
- D. A nature reserve

**9.** Which is an assumption of the mark-release-recapture method?
- A. The marked individuals do not mix back into the population
- B. There is significant migration during the study
- C. Marking does not affect the survival or behaviour of the animals
- D. The population doubles between samples

**10.** A community has 4 species, each represented by exactly the same number of individuals. Compared to a community with 4 species where one species makes up 90% of individuals, the first community has:
- A. Lower species richness
- B. Higher species evenness and higher diversity
- C. Lower diversity
- D. The same diversity

## Part C / D — 应用与实验数据题 Application & Practical（仿 Paper 4/5）

**1.** A student sampled the invertebrates in a stream and recorded the following data:

| Species | Number of individuals (n) |
|---|---|
| Mayfly nymph | 12 |
| Caddisfly larva | 8 |
| Freshwater shrimp | 15 |
| Water beetle | 5 |

   (a) Calculate Simpson's Index of Diversity (D = 1 − Σ(n/N)²) for this community. Show your working. **[4]**

   (b) A second, polluted stream gave a value of D = 0.21. Compare the biodiversity of the two streams and suggest what the difference indicates about the effect of pollution. **[3]**

**2.** A scientist estimated the population of snails in a garden using mark-release-recapture. In the first capture, 60 snails were marked and released. In the second capture, 75 snails were caught, of which 20 were marked.

   (a) Estimate the total snail population. Show your working. **[2]**

   (b) State **two** assumptions that must be true for this estimate to be valid. **[2]**

   (c) Suggest why marking snails with a bright paint could lead to an overestimate of the population size. **[2]**

**3.** A student wants to investigate how the distribution of a plant species changes from the edge of a woodland into open grassland.

   (a) Suggest which sampling method would be most appropriate, and justify your choice. **[2]**

   (b) State **two** environmental (abiotic) factors the student could measure along the gradient. **[2]**
