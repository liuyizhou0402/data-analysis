## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which of the following is a source of heritable variation?
- A. Differences in diet between individuals
- B. Mutation and meiosis
- C. Tanning of skin after sun exposure
- D. Muscle growth from exercise

**2.** In a population of birds, individuals with intermediate beak size survive better than those with very large or very small beaks. This is an example of:
- A. Directional selection
- B. Disruptive selection
- C. Stabilising selection
- D. Artificial selection

**3.** The overuse of antibiotics has led to populations of bacteria that are mostly resistant. This is an example of:
- A. Stabilising selection
- B. Directional selection
- C. Lamarckian inheritance
- D. Genetic drift

**4.** In the Hardy-Weinberg equation, what does the term 2pq represent?
- A. The frequency of the dominant allele
- B. The frequency of homozygous recessive individuals
- C. The frequency of heterozygous individuals
- D. The frequency of the recessive allele

**5.** A recessive genetic condition occurs in 1 in 2500 people. Using the Hardy-Weinberg principle, what is the frequency of the recessive allele (q)?
- A. 0.0004
- B. 0.02
- C. 0.04
- D. 0.2

**6.** Which condition is NOT required for a population to remain in Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium?
- A. Random mating
- B. No natural selection
- C. A small population size
- D. No migration

**7.** Two populations of a species become separated by a mountain range and, over many generations, can no longer interbreed to produce fertile offspring. This is an example of:
- A. Sympatric speciation
- B. Allopatric speciation
- C. Artificial selection
- D. Stabilising selection

**8.** Which statement about natural selection is correct?
- A. Organisms develop adaptations because they need them
- B. Variation arises randomly, and selection acts on it non-randomly
- C. Acquired characteristics are passed to offspring
- D. Individual organisms evolve during their lifetime

**9.** A mule (offspring of a horse and a donkey) is sterile. This is an example of:
- A. A prezygotic isolation mechanism
- B. A postzygotic isolation mechanism
- C. Sympatric speciation
- D. Artificial selection

**10.** Which is an example of artificial selection?
- A. The evolution of antibiotic resistance in bacteria
- B. The development of high-yield wheat varieties by farmers
- C. The camouflage colouration of peppered moths
- D. The long neck of giraffes

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** Cystic fibrosis is an autosomal recessive condition. In a population, 1 in 2025 babies is born with cystic fibrosis.

   (a) Use the Hardy-Weinberg principle to calculate the frequency of the recessive allele (q). Show your working. **[2]**

   (b) Calculate the frequency of the dominant allele (p). **[1]**

   (c) Calculate the percentage of the population who are carriers (heterozygous). Show your working. **[2]**

   (d) Explain why the recessive allele remains relatively common in the population despite cystic fibrosis being a serious condition. **[2]**

**2.** A population of insects is exposed to a pesticide. The graph shows the percentage of insects surviving exposure over several generations.

*(Assume survival starts at ~5% in generation 1 and rises to ~85% by generation 8.)*

   (a) Describe the change in the percentage of insects surviving over the generations, quoting data. **[2]**

   (b) Explain, in terms of natural selection, why the percentage of surviving insects increased. **[4]**

   (c) Suggest **one** strategy farmers could use to reduce the development of pesticide resistance. **[1]**

**3.** Scientists studied two populations of squirrels living on opposite rims of a large canyon. The populations look slightly different and never interbreed.

   (a) Suggest how these two populations may have formed from a single ancestral population. **[3]**

   (b) Explain what evidence would be needed to confirm that the two populations are now separate species. **[2]**
