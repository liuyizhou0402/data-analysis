## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) How natural selection changes allele frequency. [5]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. There is **genetic variation** in the population (due to mutation). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. More offspring are produced than can survive → **competition / struggle for survival**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Individuals with **advantageous alleles** are better adapted (selection pressure). **[1]**
- ✓ 4. These individuals are **more likely to survive and reproduce**, passing on the advantageous allele. **[1]**
- ✓ 5. Over generations, the **frequency of the advantageous allele increases** in the population. **[1]**

**(b) Directional vs stabilising selection. [4]** — *Distinguish + examples*
- ✓ 1. Directional: favours **one extreme** phenotype → mean shifts. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Example: antibiotic resistance / longer giraffe necks. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Stabilising: favours the **intermediate** phenotype, selects against extremes. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Example: human birth weight. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Hardy-Weinberg equation + terms. [3]**
- ✓ 1. Equation: **p² + 2pq + q² = 1** (and p + q = 1). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. p² = frequency of **homozygous dominant**; q² = frequency of **homozygous recessive**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. 2pq = frequency of **heterozygous** individuals. **[1]**

**(b) Three conditions for equilibrium. [3]**
任三条：
- ✓ Large population (no genetic drift). **[1]**
- ✓ No mutation. **[1]**
- ✓ No migration (gene flow). **[1]**
- ✓ Random mating. **[1]**
- ✓ No natural selection. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Define speciation. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The formation of a **new species**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. From existing populations that become **reproductively isolated** (can no longer interbreed to produce fertile offspring). **[1]**

**(b) Allopatric speciation. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. A population is separated by a **geographical barrier** (e.g. mountain, sea). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The two populations experience **different selection pressures** / undergo independent mutations. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Allele frequencies / gene pools **diverge** over many generations. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. They become so different that they **can no longer interbreed** to produce fertile offspring → separate species. **[1]**

**(c) Sympatric speciation. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Occurs **without geographical isolation** (in the same area). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Reproductive isolation arises through e.g. **different breeding times, behaviours, or polyploidy**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. This prevents interbreeding, so gene pools diverge → new species. **[1]**

---

### B4
**(a) Natural vs artificial selection. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. In natural selection, the **environment** determines which individuals survive and reproduce. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. In artificial selection, **humans** choose which individuals breed (for desired traits). **[1]**

**(b) Two disadvantages of artificial selection. [2]** — *Suggest*
任两条：
- ✓ Reduced **genetic diversity** / narrow gene pool. **[1]**
- ✓ **Inbreeding depression** / increased susceptibility to disease. **[1]**
- ✓ Loss of potentially useful alleles. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Calculate q. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ 1. q² = 1/2025 = 0.000494. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. q = √0.000494 = **0.0222 (≈ 0.022)**. **[1]**

**(b) Calculate p. [1]**
- ✓ p = 1 − q = 1 − 0.022 = **0.978**. **[1]**

**(c) Percentage carriers. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ 1. Carriers = 2pq = 2 × 0.978 × 0.022. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. = 0.043 = **4.3%**. **[1]**

**(d) Why recessive allele remains common. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. **Carriers (heterozygotes) are unaffected** and do not get selected against. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. They carry and pass on the recessive allele without showing the condition, so it remains in the gene pool (carriers may even have a selective advantage). **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Describe change in survival. [2]** — *Describe（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. The percentage surviving **increased** over the generations. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. From about **5% in generation 1 to ~85% by generation 8**. **[1]**

**(b) Explain increase by natural selection. [4]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. There was genetic **variation**; some insects had a **resistance allele** (from random mutation). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. When the pesticide was applied, **non-resistant insects were killed**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Resistant insects **survived and reproduced**, passing on the resistance allele. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Over generations, the **frequency of the resistance allele increased** → higher survival (directional selection). **[1]**

**(c) Strategy to reduce resistance. [1]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ Any one: **rotate / alternate different pesticides** ／ use pesticides only when necessary ／ use **integrated pest management** (biological control). **[1]**

### C3
**(a) How two populations formed. [3]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. An ancestral population was **divided by the canyon** (geographical barrier). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The two populations faced **different selection pressures / mutations / genetic drift**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Their gene pools **diverged** over time (allopatric speciation). **[1]**

**(b) Evidence they are separate species. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Show that, if brought together, they **cannot interbreed** (or do not breed in the wild). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Or that any offspring produced would be **infertile** (no fertile offspring = separate species). **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
- H-W 计算（C1）是必拿分：务必按"q² → 开平方得 q → p=1−q → 2pq"的链条，**最易丢的是忘记开平方**（直接把 q² 当 q）。
- B1(a) 自然选择 5 分题是模板题：变异→过度繁殖→选择压力→适者生存繁殖→频率改变，五步一一对应。
- C2(b) 抗性进化：强调"突变**预先存在**"——不能写"昆虫为了生存而产生抗性"（拉马克错误，0 分）。
- 强调"种群进化、个体不进化"——这是 explain 题的得分关键。
