## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** In which part of a cell does glycolysis take place?
- A. Mitochondrial matrix
- B. Inner mitochondrial membrane
- C. Cytoplasm
- D. Intermembrane space

**2.** What is the net yield of ATP produced directly (by substrate-level phosphorylation) during glycolysis of one glucose molecule?
- A. 1 ATP
- B. 2 ATP
- C. 4 ATP
- D. 38 ATP

**3.** Where does the Krebs cycle take place?
- A. Cytoplasm
- B. Mitochondrial matrix
- C. Inner mitochondrial membrane
- D. Outer mitochondrial membrane

**4.** During chemiosmosis, what is the immediate source of energy used by ATP synthase to produce ATP?
- A. The oxidation of glucose
- B. The movement of H⁺ ions down an electrochemical gradient
- C. The reduction of oxygen to water
- D. The transfer of electrons between carriers

**5.** What is the role of oxygen in aerobic respiration?
- A. It is the substrate for the Krebs cycle
- B. It is the final electron acceptor at the end of the electron transport chain
- C. It directly produces ATP by substrate-level phosphorylation
- D. It is reduced to carbon dioxide

**6.** Why is the yield of ATP from one molecule of FADH₂ lower than that from one molecule of NADH?
- A. FADH₂ carries fewer electrons than NADH
- B. FADH₂ delivers its electrons at a later point in the chain (complex II), so fewer H⁺ are pumped
- C. FADH₂ is produced only in the cytoplasm
- D. FADH₂ cannot be used in oxidative phosphorylation

**7.** What is the main purpose of anaerobic respiration (fermentation) in muscle cells?
- A. To produce large amounts of ATP
- B. To produce lactate as a useful energy source
- C. To regenerate NAD⁺ so that glycolysis can continue
- D. To produce carbon dioxide for gas exchange

**8.** A germinating seed has a respiratory quotient (RQ) of 0.7. Which respiratory substrate is most likely being used?
- A. Carbohydrate
- B. Protein
- C. Lipid
- D. A mixture of carbohydrate and protein

**9.** Which products are formed when yeast respires anaerobically?
- A. Lactate and NAD⁺
- B. Ethanol, carbon dioxide and NAD⁺
- C. Pyruvate and ATP
- D. Acetyl-CoA and CO₂

**10.** Cyanide inhibits the final electron carrier (cytochrome oxidase) in the electron transport chain. What is the immediate effect on a cell?
- A. Glycolysis stops immediately
- B. The proton gradient cannot be maintained, so oxidative phosphorylation stops
- C. The Krebs cycle speeds up to compensate
- D. ATP production by substrate-level phosphorylation stops

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** A student investigated the effect of a metabolic poison (compound X) on isolated mitochondria. Compound X makes the inner mitochondrial membrane permeable (leaky) to H⁺ ions.

   (a) Predict and explain the effect of compound X on the rate of ATP synthesis. **[3]**

   (b) The student observed that, after adding compound X, oxygen consumption by the mitochondria actually increased, and the temperature of the suspension rose. Explain these observations. **[3]**

**2.** The table shows the volume of oxygen consumed and carbon dioxide produced by germinating seeds of two species over one hour.

| Species | O₂ consumed / cm³ | CO₂ produced / cm³ |
|---|---|---|
| Pea | 12.0 | 12.0 |
| Sunflower | 15.0 | 10.5 |

   (a) Calculate the respiratory quotient (RQ) for each species. Show your working. **[2]**

   (b) Using the RQ values, identify the respiratory substrate most likely being used by each species. Explain your answer. **[3]**

   (c) The sunflower seed stores large amounts of oil. Explain how this is consistent with your answer to (b). **[1]**

**3.** During a 100 m sprint, an athlete's muscles rely heavily on anaerobic respiration.

   (a) Explain why anaerobic respiration is important during a short, intense sprint. **[2]**

   (b) After the sprint, the athlete continues to breathe heavily for several minutes ("oxygen debt"). Explain why. **[3]**
