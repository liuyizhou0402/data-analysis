## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Describe glycolysis. [5]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Glucose (6C) is **phosphorylated** using 2 ATP. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The phosphorylated 6C sugar is **split into two 3C molecules** (triose phosphate / G3P). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Each triose phosphate is **oxidised**, reducing NAD⁺ to **NADH** (2 NADH total). **[1]**
- ✓ 4. ATP is produced by **substrate-level phosphorylation** (4 ATP total). **[1]**
- ✓ 5. Net products: **2 pyruvate + net 2 ATP + 2 NADH**. **[1]**

**(b) Why glycolysis can occur anaerobically but Krebs cannot. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Glycolysis **does not require oxygen** and occurs in the cytoplasm. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The Krebs cycle requires a continuous supply of **NAD⁺ and FAD**, which are regenerated by the electron transport chain. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Without O₂ as final electron acceptor, the ETC stops, NAD⁺/FAD are not regenerated → Krebs cycle halts. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Location of Krebs cycle. [1]**
- ✓ The **mitochondrial matrix**. **[1]**

**(b) Products per turn from one acetyl-CoA. [3]**
- ✓ **3 NADH (reduced NAD)** **[1]**
- ✓ **1 FADH₂ (reduced FAD) + 1 ATP** **[1]**
- ✓ **2 CO₂** **[1]**

**(c) Role of decarboxylation and dehydrogenation. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. **Decarboxylation** removes CO₂ → carbon atoms from acetyl-CoA are released as waste. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **Dehydrogenation** removes hydrogen (electrons) → reduces NAD⁺/FAD to NADH/FADH₂, which carry electrons to the ETC. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) How H⁺ gradient is established. [3]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. NADH and FADH₂ donate electrons to carriers in the **inner mitochondrial membrane**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. As electrons pass along the chain, energy is released. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. This energy is used to **pump H⁺ from the matrix into the intermembrane space**, creating a higher H⁺ concentration there. **[1]**

**(b) How gradient synthesises ATP. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. H⁺ ions flow back into the matrix **down their electrochemical gradient**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. They pass through **ATP synthase** channels. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The energy released drives the phosphorylation of **ADP + Pᵢ → ATP** (chemiosmosis). **[1]**

**(c) Why inner membrane folded into cristae. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Cristae **increase the surface area** of the inner membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. More space for **electron carriers and ATP synthase** → more ATP produced. **[1]**

---

### B4
**(a) Compare ATP yield aerobic vs anaerobic. [2]** — *Compare*
- ✓ 1. Aerobic respiration yields **~30 (or up to 38) ATP** per glucose. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Anaerobic respiration yields only **2 ATP** per glucose. **[1]**

**(b) Biochemical reason for lower anaerobic yield. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. In anaerobic respiration, only **glycolysis** produces ATP (net 2). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The Krebs cycle and oxidative phosphorylation **do not occur** (no O₂ as electron acceptor). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Most ATP normally comes from **oxidative phosphorylation**, which is lost — NADH is used to regenerate NAD⁺ instead of producing ATP. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Effect of leaky membrane on ATP synthesis. [3]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. ATP synthesis **decreases / stops**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. H⁺ ions leak back across the membrane **without passing through ATP synthase**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The proton gradient **collapses / cannot be maintained**, so ATP synthase cannot function. **[1]**

**(b) Why O₂ consumption increased and temperature rose. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. With the gradient dissipating, the ETC runs **faster** trying to re-establish the gradient → **more O₂ consumed** as final electron acceptor. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Energy from electron transport is **not captured as ATP**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Instead it is released as **heat** → temperature rises (the poison is an "uncoupler"). **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Calculate RQ. [2]** — *Calculate*
- ✓ 1. Pea: RQ = 12.0 / 12.0 = **1.0**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Sunflower: RQ = 10.5 / 15.0 = **0.7**. **[1]**

**(b) Identify substrate from RQ. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Pea RQ = 1.0 → respiring **carbohydrate** (1 mol CO₂ produced per mol O₂ consumed). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Sunflower RQ = 0.7 → respiring **lipid/fat**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Lipids contain relatively **less oxygen** and more hydrogen, so more O₂ is needed to oxidise them → lower RQ. **[1]**

**(c) Consistency with oil storage. [1]**
- ✓ The sunflower stores **oil (lipid)**, which it uses as a respiratory substrate → consistent with RQ of 0.7. **[1]**

---

### C3
**(a) Why anaerobic respiration important in sprint. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. During intense exercise, O₂ **cannot be delivered fast enough** to muscles for aerobic respiration. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Anaerobic respiration provides ATP **rapidly** (even though in small amounts) to sustain muscle contraction. **[1]**

**(b) Why heavy breathing continues after sprint (oxygen debt). [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Lactate built up during anaerobic respiration must be **broken down/removed**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Extra O₂ is needed to **oxidise lactate** (or convert it back to glucose/glycogen in the liver). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Continued heavy breathing supplies this **extra O₂** to repay the oxygen debt. **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Why KOH (CO₂ absorber) is necessary. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. As O₂ is consumed, CO₂ is produced — if both change volume, the net volume change would be **misleading**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. KOH **absorbs the CO₂**, so any volume decrease measured is due **only to O₂ consumption**. **[1]**

**(b) Control experiment. [2]** — *Describe + Explain*
- ✓ 1. Set up an identical respirometer with **glass beads / dead organisms** of the same volume (no living organisms). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This controls for **changes in volume due to temperature/pressure** changes not related to respiration. **[1]**

**(c) Two controlled variables. [2]**
- ✓ Any two: **temperature** ／ **atmospheric pressure** ／ **mass/number of organisms** ／ time allowed ／ volume of apparatus. **[2]**

**(d) How to determine RQ. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Run the experiment **once with KOH present** (measures O₂ consumed). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Run again **without KOH** (measures net volume change = O₂ consumed − CO₂ produced); use both readings to **calculate CO₂ produced** and hence RQ = CO₂/O₂. **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
- 三个反应位置（细胞质/基质/内膜）是高频送分点，但学生常张冠李戴——务必反复强化。
- B1(a) 5 分糖酵解题：必须包含 phosphorylation（耗 ATP）、splitting、oxidation（产 NADH）、substrate-level phosphorylation（产 ATP）、净产物——这是采分点结构。
- C1 解偶联剂（uncoupler）题是经典 A* 区分题：能量以**热**形式散失、O₂ 消耗反而上升——这两点学生常想不到。
- C2 RQ 计算：脂肪 RQ ≈ 0.7 的"低"原因是脂肪含氧少、含氢多，需要更多 O₂——要能解释而非死记。
