## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which process during meiosis increases genetic variation by exchanging sections of DNA between non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes?
- A. Independent assortment
- B. Crossing over
- C. Random fertilisation
- D. DNA replication

**2.** An organism has the genotype AaBb, where the two genes are on different chromosomes. How many genetically different types of gamete can it produce (considering independent assortment only)?
- A. 2
- B. 4
- C. 8
- D. 16

**3.** A heterozygous tall plant (Tt) is crossed with a short plant (tt). What is the expected phenotypic ratio of the offspring?
- A. 3 tall : 1 short
- B. 1 tall : 1 short
- C. All tall
- D. All short

**4.** In cattle, the alleles for red coat (C^R) and white coat (C^W) are codominant; heterozygotes are roan. Two roan cattle are crossed. What is the expected phenotypic ratio of the offspring?
- A. All roan
- B. 3 red : 1 white
- C. 1 red : 2 roan : 1 white
- D. 1 red : 1 white

**5.** Red-green colour blindness is an X-linked recessive condition. A carrier woman (XᴮXᵇ) has children with a man with normal vision (XᴮY). What proportion of their sons is expected to be colour blind?
- A. 0
- B. 1/4
- C. 1/2
- D. All

**6.** In a chi-squared test, the calculated χ² value is greater than the critical value at p = 0.05. What is the correct conclusion?
- A. Accept the null hypothesis; the difference is due to chance
- B. Reject the null hypothesis; the difference is significant
- C. The observed and expected values are identical
- D. The experiment must be repeated

**7.** A base substitution mutation changes a codon from one that codes for an amino acid to a stop codon. This type of mutation is called:
- A. A silent mutation
- B. A missense mutation
- C. A nonsense mutation
- D. A frameshift mutation

**8.** In a dihybrid cross between two heterozygotes (RrYy × RrYy), an F2 ratio of 9:3:4 is observed instead of 9:3:3:1. This is most likely due to:
- A. Codominance
- B. Sex linkage
- C. Epistasis
- D. Incomplete dominance

**9.** Down syndrome (trisomy 21) is caused by:
- A. A gene mutation in chromosome 21
- B. Non-disjunction of chromosome 21 during meiosis
- C. Crossing over between chromosome 21 and 22
- D. A deletion on chromosome 21

**10.** A man with blood group AB and a woman with blood group O have children. Which blood groups are possible in their children?
- A. A, B, AB, and O
- B. Only AB
- C. A and B only
- D. O only

## Part B — 结构题 / 遗传图解 Genetic Diagrams（仿 Paper 4）

**1.** In pea plants, the allele for purple flowers (P) is dominant to the allele for white flowers (p).

   (a) A homozygous purple-flowered plant is crossed with a white-flowered plant. Draw a full genetic diagram to show the genotypes and phenotypes of the F1 generation. **[3]**

   (b) The F1 plants are allowed to self-pollinate. Draw a genetic diagram to predict the phenotypic ratio of the F2 generation. **[4]**

   (c) Explain how you could use a test cross to determine whether a purple-flowered plant is homozygous or heterozygous. **[3]**

**2.** Haemophilia is an X-linked recessive condition. Use Xᴴ to represent the normal allele and Xʰ for the haemophilia allele.

   (a) A woman who is a carrier for haemophilia has children with a man who does not have haemophilia. Draw a full genetic diagram to show the possible genotypes and phenotypes of their children. **[4]**

   (b) State the probability that a daughter of this couple will have haemophilia. Explain your answer. **[2]**

   (c) Explain why haemophilia is much more common in males than in females. **[2]**

**3.** In a species of plant, flower colour is controlled by two genes. Gene A/a and gene B/b interact (epistasis). When a plant homozygous for both dominant alleles (AABB) was crossed with one homozygous recessive (aabb), the F1 were all coloured. The F2 showed a ratio of 9 coloured : 7 white.

   (a) State what is meant by *epistasis*. **[1]**

   (b) Explain why a 9:7 ratio is observed rather than 9:3:3:1. **[3]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** In an experiment, a dihybrid cross was expected to give offspring in the ratio 9:3:3:1. The observed numbers in 160 offspring were:

| Phenotype | Observed (O) |
|---|---|
| Round, yellow | 88 |
| Round, green | 32 |
| Wrinkled, yellow | 28 |
| Wrinkled, green | 12 |

   (a) Calculate the expected number (E) of each phenotype based on the 9:3:3:1 ratio. **[2]**

   (b) Carry out a chi-squared (χ²) test on these data. Show your working. **[3]**

   (c) The critical value of χ² at p = 0.05 with 3 degrees of freedom is 7.82. State and explain your conclusion. **[2]**

**2.** The pedigree shows the inheritance of a genetic condition through three generations.

*(Assume: the condition appears in both sexes; two unaffected parents have an affected child — indicating a recessive allele; not skipping with X-linked pattern.)*

   (a) State, with a reason, whether the condition is caused by a dominant or recessive allele. **[2]**

   (b) Explain whether the data are consistent with the allele being autosomal (not sex-linked). **[2]**

   (c) A couple in the pedigree, both unaffected but each with an affected sibling, are planning a child. Estimate the probability that their child will be affected, assuming both parents are heterozygous. Show your reasoning. **[2]**
