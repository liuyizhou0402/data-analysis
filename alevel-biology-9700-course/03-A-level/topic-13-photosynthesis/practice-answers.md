## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) How chloroplast structure is adapted. [4]** — *Describe（结构→功能）*
- ✓ 1. **Thylakoid membranes** stacked into grana → large surface area for **light-harvesting pigments** (light-dependent reactions). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Thylakoid membranes contain **electron carriers and ATP synthase** for photophosphorylation. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. **Stroma** contains the enzymes (e.g. Rubisco) for the **Calvin cycle**. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Thylakoid lumen / membrane allows **proton gradient** to form for ATP synthesis. **[1]**

**(b) Precise location of Calvin cycle. [1]**
- ✓ The **stroma** of the chloroplast. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Role of light. [5]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Light is absorbed by **chlorophyll in photosystems** on the thylakoid membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Light energy **excites electrons** in chlorophyll to a higher energy level; they leave the chlorophyll. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Light causes **photolysis of water**: H₂O → 2H⁺ + ½O₂ + 2e⁻. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Electrons from photolysis **replace** those lost from PSII. **[1]**
- ✓ 5. Oxygen is released as a by-product; H⁺ is used to reduce NADP. **[1]**

**(b) How ATP is synthesised. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Excited electrons pass along the **electron transport chain** in the thylakoid membrane, releasing energy. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Energy is used to pump **H⁺ into the thylakoid lumen**, creating a proton gradient. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. H⁺ flows back through **ATP synthase**, driving ATP synthesis (photophosphorylation / chemiosmosis). **[1]**

**(c) Two uses of light-dependent products in Calvin cycle. [2]**
- ✓ 1. **ATP** provides energy for the reduction of GP to TP (and regeneration of RuBP). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **NADPH** provides reducing power (H) for the reduction of GP to TP. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Name compounds and enzyme. [3]**
- ✓ 5C compound = **RuBP (ribulose bisphosphate)**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3C compound = **GP (glycerate-3-phosphate)**. **[1]**
- ✓ Enzyme = **Rubisco**. **[1]**

**(b) Fate of triose phosphate. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Most TP is used to **regenerate RuBP** (requires ATP) to continue the cycle. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Some TP is used to synthesise **glucose / carbohydrates**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. TP can also be converted into other organic molecules (**amino acids, lipids, etc.**). **[1]**

**(c) Why Calvin cycle stops without light. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The Calvin cycle **requires ATP and NADPH**, which are produced only by the light-dependent reactions. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Without light, no ATP/NADPH is produced. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. So GP cannot be reduced to TP and RuBP cannot be regenerated → the cycle halts. **[1]**

---

### B4
**(a) Define limiting factor. [1]**
- ✓ The factor that is in **shortest supply** and so **limits the rate** of a process (when other factors are in excess). **[1]**

**(b) How increasing CO₂ increases rate at constant light. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. CO₂ is the substrate for **carbon fixation** by Rubisco (CO₂ + RuBP → GP). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. More CO₂ → more GP formed → more TP → faster Calvin cycle. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. If CO₂ was the limiting factor, increasing it increases the rate until another factor (light/temperature) becomes limiting. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Effect of light intensity at 0.04% CO₂. [2]** — *Describe（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. As light intensity increases, the rate of photosynthesis **increases** (rising part of curve). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Above a certain intensity, the rate **plateaus / levels off** (light no longer limiting). **[1]**

**(b) Why 0.4% CO₂ curve reaches higher plateau. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. At the plateau, light is no longer limiting; **CO₂ becomes the limiting factor**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. At 0.4% CO₂, there is **more CO₂ available** for carbon fixation than at 0.04%. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. So the rate can rise to a **higher maximum** before CO₂ becomes limiting again. **[1]**

**(c) Greenhouse advice on a bright day. [2]** — *Suggest + Justify*
- ✓ 1. On a bright day light is **not limiting**, so the grower should **increase the CO₂ concentration**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The graph shows higher CO₂ gives a higher rate at high light intensity (could also warm the greenhouse to optimum temperature). **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Change in GP after CO₂ reduced. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Less CO₂ → **less GP is formed** (CO₂ + RuBP → GP slows). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. GP continues to be converted to TP, so GP concentration **falls**. **[1]**

**(b) Change in RuBP after CO₂ reduced. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. RuBP is still being **regenerated** from TP but is **not being used** (less CO₂ to combine with). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. So RuBP **accumulates / increases**. **[1]**

**(c) Predict GP/RuBP if light switched off. [3]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. GP would **increase** — no ATP/NADPH to reduce GP to TP, but GP still forms from CO₂ + RuBP. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. RuBP would **decrease** — RuBP continues to be used in fixation but cannot be regenerated (needs ATP). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. This is the **opposite** of the reduced-CO₂ experiment. **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Why sodium hydrogencarbonate added. [1]**
- ✓ It provides a **source of CO₂** for photosynthesis (so CO₂ is not the limiting factor). **[1]**

**(b) How to vary light intensity in a controlled way. [2]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Place a lamp at **different measured distances** from the plant. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Use the **inverse square law** (intensity ∝ 1/distance²) to calculate relative light intensity. **[1]**

**(c) Two controlled variables. [2]**
- ✓ Any two: **temperature** (use a water bath / heat sink) ／ **CO₂ concentration** (constant hydrogencarbonate) ／ same piece of *Elodea* ／ wavelength/colour of light. **[2]**

**(d) Improvement for reliable quantitative data. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Collect the gas in a **capillary tube / gas syringe** and measure the **volume of O₂ produced** per unit time. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Repeat and calculate a **mean** to improve reliability. **[1]**

---

### D2 (Hill reaction)
**(a) Why DCPIP becomes colourless when illuminated. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. In the light, the chloroplasts carry out the **light-dependent reactions**, producing electrons (from photolysis of water). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. DCPIP acts as an **electron acceptor** (substituting for NADP); when reduced it turns from blue to **colourless**. **[1]**

**(b) Prediction in darkness. [2]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. In darkness, DCPIP would **remain blue** (no colour change). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Without light, no photolysis occurs → no electrons released → DCPIP is **not reduced**. **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips
- C2 与 B3(c) 是本主题最具区分度的推理题：断光（GP↑ RuBP↓）与降 CO₂（GP↓ RuBP↑）方向相反——务必让学生画"卡尔文循环阻断点"图来推理，而非死记。
- B2(a) 5 分题要包含：色素吸光→电子激发→光解水→电子补充→O₂/H⁺ 去向。
- Hill reaction（D2）是 Paper 5 高频实验：DCPIP 作为人工电子受体，蓝→无色 = 光反应活性指标。
- 强调术语精确："light-independent"≠"in the dark"；O₂ 来自**水**不是 CO₂。
