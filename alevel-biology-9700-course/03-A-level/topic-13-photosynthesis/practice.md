## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Where in the chloroplast do the light-dependent reactions occur?
- A. In the stroma
- B. On the thylakoid membranes
- C. In the intermembrane space
- D. On the outer chloroplast membrane

**2.** During photosynthesis, the oxygen released comes from:
- A. Carbon dioxide
- B. Glucose
- C. The photolysis of water
- D. The reduction of NADP

**3.** What are the products of the light-dependent reactions that are used in the Calvin cycle?
- A. Glucose and oxygen
- B. ATP and reduced NADP (NADPH)
- C. Carbon dioxide and water
- D. RuBP and GP

**4.** Which enzyme catalyses the fixation of carbon dioxide in the Calvin cycle?
- A. ATP synthase
- B. Dehydrogenase
- C. Rubisco
- D. Decarboxylase

**5.** In the Calvin cycle, GP (glycerate-3-phosphate) is converted to TP (triose phosphate). What is required for this reduction step?
- A. CO₂ and RuBP
- B. ATP and NADPH from the light-dependent reactions
- C. Oxygen and water
- D. Light energy directly

**6.** If a photosynthesising plant is suddenly placed in darkness, what happens to the concentrations of GP and RuBP?
- A. GP decreases, RuBP increases
- B. GP increases, RuBP decreases
- C. Both increase
- D. Both decrease

**7.** A plant is photosynthesising at a light intensity on the plateau region of a light intensity vs photosynthesis rate graph. What is the limiting factor?
- A. Light intensity
- B. Chlorophyll concentration
- C. A factor other than light (e.g. CO₂ concentration or temperature)
- D. Water availability

**8.** Why does a leaf appear green?
- A. Chlorophyll absorbs green light strongly
- B. Chlorophyll reflects green light and absorbs red and blue light
- C. Carotenoids reflect red light
- D. Green light has the highest energy

**9.** Which statement correctly compares the absorption spectrum and the action spectrum of a photosynthetic pigment?
- A. They are completely unrelated
- B. They correspond closely, showing the pigment drives photosynthesis
- C. The action spectrum shows light absorbed; the absorption spectrum shows rate of photosynthesis
- D. The absorption spectrum is always lower than the action spectrum

**10.** In cyclic photophosphorylation, which products are formed?
- A. ATP, NADPH and oxygen
- B. ATP only
- C. NADPH only
- D. Glucose and oxygen

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** The graph shows the rate of photosynthesis of a plant at two different CO₂ concentrations (0.04% and 0.4%), plotted against light intensity.

*(Both curves rise then plateau; the 0.4% CO₂ curve plateaus at a higher rate than the 0.04% curve.)*

   (a) Describe the effect of increasing light intensity on the rate of photosynthesis at 0.04% CO₂. **[2]**

   (b) Explain why the curve at 0.4% CO₂ reaches a higher plateau than the curve at 0.04% CO₂. **[3]**

   (c) A grower wants to maximise the rate of photosynthesis in a greenhouse on a bright summer day. Using the graph, suggest and justify what the grower should do. **[2]**

**2.** Scientists studied the concentration of GP and RuBP in algae. They reduced the CO₂ concentration sharply at time T and measured the two compounds before and after.

| Compound | Concentration before T | Concentration after T |
|---|---|---|
| GP | high | low |
| RuBP | low | high |

   (a) Explain the change in GP concentration after the CO₂ concentration was reduced. **[2]**

   (b) Explain the change in RuBP concentration after the CO₂ concentration was reduced. **[2]**

   (c) Predict what would happen to GP and RuBP if, instead, the light had been switched off (with CO₂ kept constant). Explain your answer. **[3]**

## Part D — 实验技能 Practical（关联 Paper 5）

   (a) Explain why sodium hydrogencarbonate is added to the water. **[1]**

   (b) Describe how the student could vary light intensity in a controlled way. **[2]**

   (c) State **two** variables that must be controlled in this experiment. **[2]**

   (d) Counting bubbles is described as an unreliable measure of photosynthesis rate. Suggest **one** improvement to obtain more reliable, quantitative data. **[2]**

**2.** In the Hill reaction, isolated chloroplasts are mixed with DCPIP, a blue dye that becomes colourless when reduced. The mixture is illuminated.

   (a) Explain why the DCPIP becomes colourless when the chloroplasts are illuminated. **[2]**

   (b) Predict the result if the experiment were carried out in darkness, and explain your prediction. **[2]**
