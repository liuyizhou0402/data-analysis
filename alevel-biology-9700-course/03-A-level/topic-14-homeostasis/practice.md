## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** Which statement best describes negative feedback?
- A. A change in a variable triggers a response that amplifies the change
- B. A change in a variable triggers a response that returns it toward the set point
- C. The internal environment is allowed to change freely
- D. Two hormones are always released simultaneously

**2.** What provides the high hydrostatic pressure required for ultrafiltration in the glomerulus?
- A. Active transport of water into the Bowman's capsule
- B. The afferent arteriole being wider than the efferent arteriole
- C. ADH increasing the permeability of the collecting duct
- D. The contraction of podocytes

**3.** Which substances are normally NOT present in the glomerular filtrate of a healthy person?
- A. Glucose and amino acids
- B. Urea and water
- C. Plasma proteins and blood cells
- D. Sodium ions and chloride ions

**4.** In the proximal convoluted tubule (PCT), glucose is reabsorbed by:
- A. Simple diffusion only
- B. Osmosis
- C. Co-transport with sodium ions (secondary active transport)
- D. Ultrafiltration

**5.** What is the effect of ADH on the kidney?
- A. It decreases the permeability of the collecting duct to water
- B. It increases the reabsorption of water from the collecting duct, producing concentrated urine
- C. It increases the rate of ultrafiltration in the glomerulus
- D. It stimulates active transport of glucose in the PCT

**6.** Which cells secrete insulin, and what is its effect on blood glucose?
- A. α cells; increases blood glucose
- B. β cells; decreases blood glucose
- C. β cells; increases blood glucose
- D. α cells; decreases blood glucose

**7.** A person has Type 1 diabetes. What is the most likely cause?
- A. Target cells have become resistant to insulin
- B. The β cells of the pancreas have been destroyed and cannot produce insulin
- C. The liver produces too much glycogen
- D. The kidneys cannot reabsorb glucose

**8.** What happens to guard cells when stomata open?
- A. K⁺ ions leave the guard cells, water leaves by osmosis, and the cells become flaccid
- B. K⁺ ions enter the guard cells, water enters by osmosis, and the cells become turgid
- C. ABA is released, causing the guard cells to lose water
- D. The guard cells lose water and bend outward

**9.** During glucagon action on liver cells, the second messenger formed is:
- A. ATP
- B. Glucose
- C. cyclic AMP (cAMP)
- D. Insulin

**10.** Which adaptation of the proximal convoluted tubule cells increases the rate of selective reabsorption?
- A. Thick walls with few mitochondria
- B. Microvilli and numerous mitochondria
- C. A waterproof Casparian strip
- D. Absence of carrier proteins

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** The table shows the relative concentrations of three substances in the blood plasma, glomerular filtrate, and urine of a healthy person.

| Substance | Plasma | Filtrate | Urine |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protein | 80 | 0 | 0 |
| Glucose | 5 | 5 | 0 |
| Urea | 0.3 | 0.3 | 20 |

   (a) Explain why protein is present in the plasma but absent from the filtrate. **[2]**

   (b) Explain why glucose is present in the filtrate but absent from the urine. **[2]**

   (c) Explain why urea is far more concentrated in the urine than in the filtrate. **[2]**

**2.** A student drinks 1 litre of water quickly. Their urine production is monitored over the next 3 hours.

   (a) Predict and explain how the volume and concentration of urine will change after drinking the water. **[3]**

   (b) Explain the role of the hypothalamus and the pituitary gland in this response. **[3]**

**3.** The graph shows blood glucose concentration over time in a healthy person and a person with untreated Type 1 diabetes, both after eating a meal.

*(Healthy: rises then returns to baseline within 2 h. Diabetic: rises higher and stays elevated.)*

   (a) Describe and explain the difference between the two curves after the meal. **[3]**

   (b) Suggest why the diabetic person's blood glucose eventually causes glucose to appear in their urine. **[2]**
