## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) Negative feedback and importance. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. A change in a variable away from the set point is **detected by receptors**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. A response is triggered that **reverses the change**, returning the variable toward the set point. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Important because it keeps internal conditions stable (e.g. for **enzyme activity / cell function**). **[1]**

**(b) Response to falling body temperature. [3]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Hypothalamus detects temperature drop → sends signals to effectors. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **Shivering** (muscle contraction generates heat) and **vasoconstriction** of skin arterioles (reduces heat loss). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Less sweating / hairs raised (erector muscles) → reduces heat loss → temperature rises back to set point. **[1]**

---

### B2
**(a) Ultrafiltration in glomerulus. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Blood enters the glomerulus via the **afferent arteriole** (wider than the efferent), creating **high hydrostatic pressure**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This forces small molecules out of the blood through the capillary fenestrations. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The filtrate passes through the **basement membrane** (acts as a molecular sieve) and gaps between **podocytes**. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Small molecules (water, glucose, urea, ions) enter the Bowman's capsule; **large molecules (proteins, blood cells) remain in the blood**. **[1]**

**(b) PCT adaptations for reabsorption. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. **Microvilli** increase surface area for reabsorption. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Many **mitochondria** provide ATP for active transport. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Many **carrier/co-transporter proteins** in the membrane for glucose and ion uptake. **[1]**

**(c) Glucose absent from urine but present in diabetic urine. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Normally **all glucose is reabsorbed** in the PCT by co-transport → none in urine. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. In an untreated diabetic, blood glucose is **very high**, so filtrate glucose is very high. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The carrier proteins become **saturated** → not all glucose can be reabsorbed → glucose appears in urine. **[1]**

---

### B3
**(a) Loop of Henle + collecting duct producing concentrated urine. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. The **ascending limb** actively pumps Na⁺/Cl⁻ out into the medulla; it is **impermeable to water**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This makes the **medulla increasingly concentrated** (low water potential) toward the bottom. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The **descending limb** is permeable to water, so water leaves the filtrate by osmosis. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. As the **collecting duct** passes through the concentrated medulla, water is reabsorbed by osmosis → concentrated urine. **[1]**

**(b) Role of ADH when dehydrated. [4]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Dehydration → blood water potential **falls** → detected by **osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The **posterior pituitary releases more ADH** into the blood. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. ADH increases the permeability of the **collecting duct** by inserting **aquaporins (water channels)**. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. More water is **reabsorbed** → smaller volume of concentrated urine → blood water potential returns to normal. **[1]**

---

### B4
**(a) Role of insulin in reducing blood glucose. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. β cells detect high blood glucose and **secrete insulin**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Insulin causes liver and muscle cells to **take up more glucose** (more GLUT4 transporters in membrane). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Insulin stimulates conversion of glucose to **glycogen (glycogenesis)** in liver/muscle. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Increases the rate of glucose use in respiration → blood glucose falls to normal. **[1]**

**(b) Cause of Type 1 vs Type 2. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Type 1: **β cells destroyed** (autoimmune) → little or no insulin produced. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Type 2: target cells become **resistant** to insulin / fewer functional insulin receptors. **[1]**

**(c) Why inject insulin not take as tablet. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Insulin is a **protein**, so it would be **digested** (hydrolysed by proteases) in the stomach/gut. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. It would not reach the blood intact, so it must be **injected** directly. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Protein in plasma but not filtrate. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Plasma proteins are **too large** to pass through the basement membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. So they are **not filtered** into the Bowman's capsule → absent from filtrate. **[1]**

**(b) Glucose in filtrate but not urine. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Glucose is small enough to be **filtered** into the filtrate. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. It is **completely reabsorbed** in the PCT (co-transport) → absent from urine. **[1]**

**(c) Urea more concentrated in urine than filtrate. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. As filtrate passes along the nephron, **water is reabsorbed** back into the blood. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Urea is largely **not reabsorbed**, so it becomes far more concentrated as water is removed. **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Predict urine after drinking water. [3]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. Urine **volume increases** and urine becomes **more dilute**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Drinking water raises blood water potential → less ADH released. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Collecting duct becomes **less permeable to water** → less water reabsorbed → dilute urine. **[1]**

**(b) Role of hypothalamus and pituitary. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. **Osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus** detect the increased blood water potential. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The **posterior pituitary** reduces its secretion of ADH. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Lower ADH → fewer aquaporins in collecting duct → less water reabsorbed (negative feedback). **[1]**

---

### C3
**(a) Difference between curves after meal. [3]** — *Describe + Explain（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. Both rise after the meal, but the diabetic's blood glucose rises **higher** and **stays elevated** while the healthy person's returns to baseline within ~2 h. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The healthy person secretes **insulin**, which lowers blood glucose (uptake + glycogenesis). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The Type 1 diabetic produces **no insulin**, so glucose is not taken up/stored → remains high. **[1]**

**(b) Why glucose appears in diabetic's urine. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Blood glucose is so high that filtrate glucose **exceeds the reabsorption capacity** of the PCT. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Carrier proteins are **saturated**, so excess glucose remains in the filtrate and appears in urine. **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

### D1
**(a) Method to measure stomatal opening. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Make a **nail varnish / clear impression** of the leaf surface (epidermal peel). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. View under a microscope and **measure stomatal pore width** with a calibrated eyepiece graticule. **[1]**

**(b) Two controlled variables. [2]**
- ✓ Any two: **temperature** ／ **humidity** ／ **CO₂ concentration** ／ same plant species / same leaf age ／ time of day. **[2]**

**(c) Physiological basis for stomata opening in light. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. In light, guard cells actively **pump in K⁺**, lowering their water potential. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Water enters by **osmosis** → guard cells become **turgid** and bend apart → stomata open. **[1]**

**(d) Investigate effect of ABA. [2]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ 1. Apply **ABA solution** (various concentrations) to leaves/epidermal peels and measure stomatal pore width. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Compare with a **control** (no ABA / water only) under the same conditions. **[1]**
