## Part B — 结构题（按采分点 ✓ 拆解）

### B1
**(a) How resting potential established. [4]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. The **Na⁺/K⁺ pump** actively transports **3 Na⁺ out and 2 K⁺ in** (using ATP). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. This creates concentration gradients: high Na⁺ outside, high K⁺ inside. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The membrane is **more permeable to K⁺** (more K⁺ channels), so K⁺ diffuses out. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. Loss of positive charge + large organic anions inside → inside is **negative (~−70 mV)**. **[1]**

**(b) Ionic events during action potential. [4]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Stimulus reaches threshold → **voltage-gated Na⁺ channels open** → Na⁺ rushes in (depolarisation to +40 mV). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. At peak, Na⁺ channels **close (inactivate)**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. **Voltage-gated K⁺ channels open** → K⁺ moves out (repolarisation). **[1]**
- ✓ 4. K⁺ outflow briefly overshoots → hyperpolarisation; Na⁺/K⁺ pump restores resting potential. **[1]**

**(c) Importance of refractory period. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Ensures action potentials travel in **one direction only** (the region behind cannot re-fire). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Limits the **frequency** of action potentials (so they are discrete / separate). **[1]**

### B2
**(a) Transmission across cholinergic synapse. [6]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. Action potential arrives at the **presynaptic membrane**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **Voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels open** → Ca²⁺ enters the presynaptic neurone. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Ca²⁺ causes **synaptic vesicles to fuse** with the presynaptic membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. **Acetylcholine is released by exocytosis** into the synaptic cleft and diffuses across. **[1]**
- ✓ 5. ACh **binds to receptors** on the postsynaptic membrane → ligand-gated Na⁺ channels open → depolarisation. **[1]**
- ✓ 6. If threshold is reached, a **new action potential** is generated; ACh is broken down by **acetylcholinesterase**. **[1]**

**(b) Why one direction only. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Neurotransmitter (vesicles) is only present in/released from the **presynaptic neurone**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Receptors are only on the **postsynaptic membrane** → signal can only pass pre → post. **[1]**

### B3
**(a) Structure of a sarcomere. [3]** — *Describe*
- ✓ 1. A sarcomere is the region between two **Z lines**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. The **A band** contains thick (myosin) filaments (and overlapping actin); the **I band** contains only thin (actin) filaments. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The **H zone** (centre of A band) contains only myosin; thin filaments are attached to the Z lines. **[1]**

**(b) Role of Ca²⁺ and ATP in sliding filament. [5]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Ca²⁺ is released from the **sarcoplasmic reticulum** when the muscle is stimulated. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Ca²⁺ binds to **troponin**, causing **tropomyosin to move** and expose myosin-binding sites on actin. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Myosin heads bind to actin forming **cross-bridges**; the power stroke pulls actin toward the centre. **[1]**
- ✓ 4. **ATP binds** to the myosin head, causing it to **detach** from actin. **[1]**
- ✓ 5. ATP is hydrolysed (ATPase) → myosin head **re-cocks** to repeat the cycle. **[1]**

**(c) Width changes on contraction. [3]**
- ✓ A band: **no change**. **[1]**
- ✓ I band: **decreases (narrows)**. **[1]**
- ✓ H zone: **decreases (narrows)**. **[1]**

### B4
**(a) Compare nervous and hormonal control. [3]** — *Compare*
- ✓ 1. Speed: nervous is **fast** (ms); hormonal is **slower** (seconds–days). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Duration: nervous effects are **short-lived**; hormonal effects are **longer-lasting**. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Signal: nervous uses **electrical impulses (+ chemical at synapses)**; hormonal uses **chemicals (hormones) in blood**. **[1]**

**(b) How auxin causes shoot to bend toward light. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Auxin (IAA) is **redistributed to the shaded side** of the shoot. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Higher auxin concentration on the shaded side **stimulates cell elongation** there. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. The shaded side grows faster/longer than the lit side → shoot **bends toward the light**. **[1]**

## Part C — 应用/数据题

### C1
**(a) Compare A and B conduction velocities. [3]** — *Compare + Explain（引用数据）*
- ✓ 1. Axon A (120 m s⁻¹) conducts **much faster** than axon B (10 m s⁻¹), despite same diameter. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Axon A is **myelinated** → saltatory conduction (impulse jumps between nodes of Ranvier). **[1]**
- ✓ 3. Axon B is unmyelinated → depolarisation must occur **continuously along the whole membrane** → slower. **[1]**

**(b) Difference between B and C. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Axon B (20 μm, 10 m s⁻¹) is faster than C (5 μm, 2 m s⁻¹). **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **Larger diameter** axons have less resistance to ion flow → faster conduction. **[1]**

**(c) Advantage of myelination. [1]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ Faster responses / reflexes ／ allows more axons in a smaller space ／ energy-efficient conduction. **[1]**

---

### C2
**(a) Cause of rise −70 to +40 mV. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Voltage-gated **Na⁺ channels open**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **Na⁺ rushes in** down its electrochemical gradient → membrane depolarises to +40 mV. **[1]**

**(b) Cause of return +40 toward −70 mV. [2]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Na⁺ channels close; **voltage-gated K⁺ channels open**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. **K⁺ moves out** of the axon → membrane repolarises. **[1]**

**(c) Drug blocks K⁺ channels — effect. [2]** — *Predict + Explain*
- ✓ 1. Repolarisation would be **slowed / prevented**. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. Without K⁺ efflux, the membrane stays depolarised longer → action potential is **prolonged**. **[1]**

### C3
**(a) Effect of curare on muscle contraction. [3]** — *Explain*
- ✓ 1. Curare binds to **ACh receptors** on the postsynaptic (muscle) membrane, blocking them. **[1]**
- ✓ 2. ACh released cannot bind → **no depolarisation** of the muscle membrane. **[1]**
- ✓ 3. No action potential in the muscle → **no contraction** (paralysis). **[1]**

**(b) Why curare doesn't affect ACh release. [1]** — *Suggest*
- ✓ Curare acts on the **postsynaptic receptors**, not on the presynaptic mechanism (Ca²⁺ influx / vesicle fusion still occur). **[1]**

## Part D — 实验技能

- ✓ Independent: **direction of light** (unilateral vs all-round / none). **[1]**

## 老师讲评建议 / Marking tips

- B3(c) A 带不变是必考送分点——务必让学生记牢：肌丝长度不变，只是滑动重叠改变。
