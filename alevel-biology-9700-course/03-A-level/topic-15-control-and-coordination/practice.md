## Part A — 选择题 Multiple Choice（仿 Paper 1，每题 1 分）

**1.** What maintains the resting potential of a neurone?
- A. Voltage-gated sodium channels being open
- B. The Na⁺/K⁺ pump and the membrane being more permeable to K⁺
- C. The continuous influx of sodium ions
- D. Acetylcholine binding to receptors

**2.** During the depolarisation phase of an action potential, which ion movement occurs?
- A. K⁺ moves out of the axon
- B. Na⁺ moves into the axon through voltage-gated channels
- C. Na⁺ is pumped out of the axon
- D. Cl⁻ moves into the axon

**3.** The "all-or-nothing" principle means that:
- A. A stronger stimulus produces a larger action potential
- B. An action potential occurs only if the threshold is reached, and is always the same size
- C. Action potentials can travel in both directions
- D. The refractory period prevents all action potentials

**4.** Why does saltatory conduction increase the speed of nerve impulse transmission?
- A. The myelin sheath generates extra action potentials
- B. The action potential "jumps" between nodes of Ranvier, so depolarisation occurs at fewer points
- C. Myelin increases the diameter of the axon
- D. Myelin pumps sodium ions faster

**5.** What is the role of calcium ions (Ca²⁺) in synaptic transmission?
- A. They bind to receptors on the postsynaptic membrane
- B. They trigger the fusion of vesicles with the presynaptic membrane, releasing neurotransmitter
- C. They break down acetylcholine
- D. They maintain the resting potential

**6.** What ensures that transmission across a synapse occurs in one direction only?
- A. The refractory period of the presynaptic neurone
- B. Neurotransmitter is only released from the presynaptic membrane and receptors are only on the postsynaptic membrane
- C. Acetylcholinesterase is found only in the synaptic cleft
- D. Calcium ions only enter the postsynaptic neurone

**7.** During muscle contraction, which band of the sarcomere does NOT change in width?
- A. I band
- B. H zone
- C. A band
- D. The distance between Z lines

**8.** What is the role of troponin in muscle contraction?
- A. It forms the cross-bridges with actin
- B. It binds calcium ions, causing tropomyosin to move and expose binding sites on actin
- C. It hydrolyses ATP to provide energy
- D. It releases calcium ions from the sarcoplasmic reticulum

**9.** A pesticide inhibits acetylcholinesterase. What is the most likely effect at a synapse?
- A. No neurotransmitter is released
- B. Acetylcholine remains in the cleft, causing continuous stimulation of the postsynaptic membrane
- C. The resting potential cannot be restored
- D. Calcium channels remain closed

**10.** A plant shoot grows toward a light source coming from one side. This is because:
- A. Auxin is destroyed on the illuminated side
- B. Auxin moves to the shaded side, causing increased cell elongation there
- C. Auxin causes cells on the illuminated side to elongate faster
- D. Light directly causes cells to bend toward it

## Part C — 应用/数据题 Application（强化 AO2）

**1.** The table shows the conduction velocity of nerve impulses in three different axons.

| Axon | Myelinated? | Diameter / μm | Conduction velocity / m s⁻¹ |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Yes | 20 | 120 |
| B | No | 20 | 10 |
| C | No | 5 | 2 |

   (a) Compare the conduction velocities of axons A and B, and explain the difference. **[3]**

   (b) Explain the difference in conduction velocity between axons B and C. **[2]**

   (c) Suggest an advantage to a mammal of having myelinated neurones. **[1]**

**2.** A scientist records the membrane potential of a neurone during stimulation. The graph shows a rise from −70 mV to +40 mV and back.

   (a) Explain what causes the rapid rise from −70 mV to +40 mV. **[2]**

   (b) Explain what causes the return from +40 mV back toward −70 mV. **[2]**

   (c) The scientist applies a drug that blocks voltage-gated potassium channels. Predict and explain the effect on the action potential. **[2]**

**3.** Curare is a poison that binds to acetylcholine receptors on the postsynaptic membrane at neuromuscular junctions, without activating them.

   (a) Explain the effect of curare on muscle contraction. **[3]**

   (b) Suggest why curare does not affect the release of acetylcholine from the presynaptic neurone. **[1]**
